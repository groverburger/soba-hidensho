### ◯ 三猿金泉録序 — *Preface to the Sanen Kinsen Roku*

The Great Ultimate moves, and gives birth to yang; when motion reaches
its extreme, it turns to stillness. In stillness it gives birth to
yin; when stillness reaches its extreme, it moves once more. The
Great Ultimate, yin and yang — these are the beginning of heaven,
earth, and the ten thousand things. One motion and one stillness are
their root.

The rising and falling of rice turns in just this way, like heaven
and earth, like yin and yang. When the force of the bull side shows
itself and the price climbs very high, the principle reaches its
extreme — and within that extreme is already contained the principle
of the bear. When the force of the bear side shows itself and the
price falls very low, within that low is already contained the
principle of the bull. When the ten thousand are weak in spirit,
that is the very principle by which rice must rise. When the many
are strong in spirit, that is the very seed by which rice must fall.
All of this is *the principle beyond principle — the principle of
nature itself* (*tensei rigai no ri*, 天性理外の理).

From the years of my prime I set my heart on the rice trade —

## Page 4 — The three-monkey doctrine and the doctrine of "empty principle"

— turning over my contrivances night and day, sending sixty years of
months and days past me, and only gradually opening my understanding
of the bullishness and bearishness of rice. From that understanding I
have drawn a fixed method of rice trading, and compiled a single
volume of secret writing which I call **三猿金泉録** (*Sanen Kinsen
Roku*).

The grain of rice itself — round in the middle, tapered at each end
— takes its form from yin and yang: the round is yang, the tapered
is yin. Receiving the energies of heaven, earth, yin, and yang, it
supplies the four classes — scholar, farmer, artisan, merchant
[OCR: `士略 … 四民工商` → likely `士農工商 四民`] — and is therefore
the single foremost treasure under heaven.

**三猿** (*san'en*) — *the three monkeys*:

The three are the *see-no* monkey, the *hear-no* monkey, and the
*speak-no* monkey.

1. When the eye sees a bullish movement, do not let the heart sink
   into reckoning that bullishness. Hold in the heart only: *this
   one should sell.*
2. When the ear hears a bearish movement, do not let the heart sink
   into the pool of that bearishness. Hold in the heart only: *this
   one should buy.*
3. Whatever bullish or bearish movement you see or hear, do not
   speak of it to others. Words mislead the hearts of men. This is
   the secret of the three monkeys.

**金泉録** (*Kinsen Roku*) — *Register of the Golden Spring*: the
name of this book.

### ◯ 高安の理は空理 — *The principle of high and low is empty principle*

The principle of high and low is *empty principle* (*kūri*, 空理): it
is not visible to the eye; it has neither shadow nor form; the body
of nothing is its body.

*Considering the sense of the opening verse:* no rule can settle in
advance when the price will rise nor when it will fall — that it will
not be fixed is exactly what "empty principle" names. Yet if you see
it purely as empty, you will find even a once-in-a-thousand-year
occasion to sell or to buy.

*Considering the sense of the closing verse:* even a thing without
shadow or form has a body; where there is a body there is a fixed
method; where there is a fixed method there is buying and selling.
The Buddhist's fixed method is the Five Precepts. The Confucian's
fixed method is the Five Constants. The Shinto fixed method is the
three virtues — **智仁勇** (*chi-jin-yū*), wisdom, benevolence,
courage. Each of them has its own fixed method.

### ◯ The fixed method of this book

When old rice is plentiful and a low price carries over to the new
rice, that is a year of **空腹上り** (*kūfuku-agari*), *empty-belly
rise*. Such a year is called an **順来の年** (*junrai no toshi*) —
a year of *orderly incoming*.
