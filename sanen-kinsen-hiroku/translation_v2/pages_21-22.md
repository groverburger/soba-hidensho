## Page 22 — Three-tier closing; opening of the five bullish secrets

*[The margin chronology continues at the top of this page and
terminates with an editor's note.]*

By the law of the three tiers, one sums up the *goodness or
badness of the year's world*: upper-tier stretches can run thirty
years; lower-tier stretches can run eight or nine years. Taking
that as given, to reckon *this year's* yield:

> Suppose the middling world's season calls for a seven-*bu*
> harvest. Multiply: **7 × 3 = 21 tawara** (the row for 7-*bu*).
> Take this 21-*tawara* figure as the *chūzumi* of the year.
> To arrive at the trading rule, add the 2-*koku* fall: the rule
> is **23 tawara**. Alternatively, if the new rice price is
> different, apply a three-*wari* fall (**6 tawara**) to the
> 20-*tawara* base and the rule becomes **26 tawara**.

> *"In the dead of year-end, this does not vary; the method has
> not a *rin*'s error."*

In any year whatever: 12-*bu* at 1-*ri* is three-*wari*; 12-*bu* at
11-*ri* is three-*wari*. *[The number-play is dense in the OCR and
the sense is that within the 3-by-5 rule, the extreme ten-*bu*
endings map to the fixed 3-*wari* ceiling.]*

By this method, in any year, *on the day of the new rice's first
sale*, take the reading as **7-*bu***.

> Editor's note: *7-*bu* is the pivot; 9-*bu* upper, 8-*bu* middle,
> 7-*bu* lower, 5-*bu* is the level-cube; 4-*bu* takes an addition
> downward.*

### ◯ 強気五ツの秘伝 — *The five secrets of the bull side*

A ladder of five falls, with a buy-size prescribed for each. The
larger the fall, the larger the buy.

| # | Fall from the pivot | Buy size   |
|---|---------------------|------------|
| 1 | 1-*koku*-and-a-half | 100 ryō    |
| 2 | 2-*koku*            | 200 ryō    |
| 3 | 2-*koku*-and-a-half | 400 ryō    |
| 4 | 3-*koku*            | 800 ryō    |
| 5 | over 3-*koku*       | 1,000 ryō  |

Editor's gloss: *"These five steps are the ten'gen (天元) trading
verse. Even two thousand ryō should be bought."*

> **Step 1 (first secret):** First, settle in the mind that for
> the year at hand the world is a 10-*bu* year and the new-rice
> price is especially high. Even if you are certain that it
> ought to drop a full three-*wari* — **never sell.**
