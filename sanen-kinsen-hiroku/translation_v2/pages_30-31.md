# Pages 30–31: Function of the bull; function of the bear; the three first secrets; closing maxims

## Page 30 — The function of the bull, concluded; the function of the bear

The seasonal catalogue from page 29 finishes here:

- (In the fifth or sixth month, long rain — a cold half-year and
  autumn) — there will be damage to follow.

On reckoning the year's world: a bear year runs at 8-*bu* on an
18-*ri* measure; a bull year runs at 7-*bu*. Drawing this out, the
outcome is a draw: a continuous **中黒り** (*naka-guroi*) —
middling-dark — strip. Such is the function of the bull.

Now a **十分の世の中** (*jippun no yo no naka*), a ten-*bu* world —
with no drought, no gale, no heavenly disaster from planting
onward — comes only three years in a hundred. Japan may be
abundant taken altogether, but east, west, south, and north, there
will always be some province hit by wind. Do not look at the one
prosperous province before you and assume the whole country is
well. As a rule, it is from the **下直なる国** (*gejiki naru kuni*,
the province with the cheapest price) that the price first rises.
Think this through.

On the buy side, **荒し物** (*suki-narashi mono*, things laid flat
by weather) are bought up, and rice too climbs — the principle is
the same. [OCR: `買方にはすきならし物などを買あげて`.]

On the sell side, there is only the natural principle, and
**nothing else** — whereas the buy side carries a 一刻の強み
(*ichi-koku no tsuyomi*). Consider this distinction carefully.

### ◯ 弱気の功 — *The function of the bear*

When the price stands very high, proclamations and sumptuary
notices issue: sweet-eyed leniency on sake, and the like; there
will also be — *[OCR square-run]* — town-wide prohibitions
(*chōjū o-kanmon*, 町中御勘門). Consider these.

Or, sometimes a wind that did not blow is claimed to have blown;
a small damage is magnified; accusations of **買〆** (*kaishime*,
buying-to-close, cornering) are made — and such things are done
*in a year to do them*. Japan is the **神国** (*shinkoku*, divine
country), and it takes justice as its principle. Heaven's natural
course ought therefore to favor the bull-side rumors of wind-and-rain
— for they have their reason. *Watch each ship, ship by ship; read
its temper.*

## Page 31 — The three first secrets; yin-and-yang rice; closing maxims

*"(Continuing from the last line of page 30) — if there is no
sand-damage and no trouble, the ship makes port. In a gale, fear of
fire makes one seal the warehouse."*

### ◯ 毎年初通段 — *The year's first-sale price*

Three ordered secrets:

1. **First secret** — take each year's **初通段** (*shottsū-dan*,
   first-sale price) as the **中墨** (*chūzumi*, reference).
2. **Second secret** — buy the *chūzumi* at once.
3. **Third secret** — stake, at the outset, a trade-mark of one
   hundred ryō against one hundred ryō of specie (*shōkin
   hyaku-ryō nite hyaku-ryō no shō-in*, 正金百両にて百両の商印).

### ◯ 陰米陽米 — *Yin rice and yang rice*

There is the distinction **陰米・陽米** (*in-mai*, *yō-mai*).
*Rising rice is yang; falling rice is yin.* The principle runs:
yin sinks into yang (*in wa yō e kuguru*); yang climbs through yin
(*yō wa in o susumi-noboru*).

### ◯ Osaka market reading

After an Osaka long-holiday, if a **松場** (*matsuba*, "pine
position") is taken and the *matsuba* stances are numerous,
*read it as a strongly-rooted rice, a good market*. If the
*matsuba* stances are few, *read it as a slack market*.

- Within the trade (*otoko-tsukaeru no uchi*, 男仕るの内), for
  each trader's own ingenuity (*kufū*) — whatever the trade, lean
  toward cutting on the **切場** (*kiriba*, cut-point).
- If the eastern and western provinces are significantly high and
  this city is also bustling — three tawara more — if the rise is
  excessive, **sell decisively**. The cheap side carries the
  reciprocal rule.
- **新米相場は他処の詮文おほま高についてよし** — *"Take the new rice's
  price from the detailed reports of other places, attending
  especially to the higher quotations."*
