### ◯ 万歳運気豊凶録 — *Ten-thousand-year record of fortune and lean*

An almanac of which sexagenary (干支) years belong to which
weather-and-harvest class. Each class is followed by the list of
years it covers within a sixty-year cycle; a short editorial note
records whether a trading rule has been set down for that class.
The classes on this page:

**暴風十二箇年** — *twelve storm years.*

Margin-gloss: *"On the twenty-sixth, rain in every locality — great
storm. The* Shiki *speaks of ‘storm, thunder, rain.’"*
Editorial rule: **二百十日三平二乗半板出染転変商内** — *at the
210th day, deploy the three-level / two-*jō* / half-*ban* / *idezome*
/ *tenpen* trade.* These are the years in which that configuration
is set up:

- 甲子 (*Kinoe-Ne*), 丁卯 (*Hinoto-U*), 丁丑 (*Hinoto-Ushi*),
  辛巳 (*Kanoto-Mi*), 甲申 (*Kinoe-Saru*), 辛卯 (*Kanoto-U*),
  甲午 (*Kinoe-Uma*), 丁酉 (*Hinoto-Tori*), 丁未 (*Hinoto-Hitsuji*),
  辛亥 (*Kanoto-I*), 甲寅 (*Kinoe-Tora*), 辛酉 (*Kanoto-Tori*).

These are *middling* years on the face of it. But with the
**木気鬱発** (*mokki uppatsu*, wood-*ki* eruption) variant-*ki* —
margin-gloss *唐陰飛木* (*tō-in hi-moku*) — if fire and wind blow
between the seventh and eighth months, the year becomes a **大凶年**
(*daikyō-nen*, a great lean year). Without that variant, it is a
good-year, *not enjoyable*.
*[OCR `変市手なけねは豊卑不楽`: the final phrase `豊卑不楽` appears
to read "an abundant-but-middling year in which profit is
absent." The reading is uncertain.]*

**洪水五箇年** — *five flood years.* Same explanation as the storm
class.

- 己巳 (*Tsuchinoto-Mi*), 己卯 (*Tsuchinoto-U*), 己亥
  (*Tsuchinoto-I*), 己酉 (*Tsuchinoto-Tori*), 壬戌 (*Mizunoe-Inu*).

With the **土気鬱発** (*doki uppatsu*, earth-*ki* eruption) variant
— margin-gloss *太陰湿土* (*taiin shitsudo*) — between the seventh
and eighth months a great flood sweeps over the fields; the year
becomes a great lean year.

**寒冷三箇年** — *three cold years.*

> Editor's aside: *"No trading rule is given here; consider
> carefully."*

- 乙丑 (*Kinoto-Ushi*), 乙未 (*Kinoto-Hitsuji*), 乙巳
  (*Kinoto-Mi*).

The cold in these years is very severe. —

## Page 16 — End of the almanac; tides; winds

Continuing the list of twenty-eight abundance years:

- 戊申 (*Tsuchinoe-Saru*), 乙卯 (*Kinoto-U*), 丙辰 (*Hinoe-Tatsu*),
  丁巳 (*Hinoto-Mi*) [OCR: `丁己巳未`], 己未 (*Tsuchinoto-Hitsuji*),
  庚申 (*Kanoe-Saru*), 癸亥 (*Mizunoto-I*).

If **勝負鬱発の変気** (*shōbu uppatsu no henki*, a "win–lose
eruption" variant-*ki*) appears in these years, the next year
follows suit. But if in the sixth or seventh month no wind blows
and the fire-heat is severe — margin-gloss *温化* (*onka*) — the
rice rots, **うんか** (*unka*, the plant-hopper) attacks; that is
the year's omen. Consider carefully.

**中豊作二箇年** — *two middling-abundance years.* Same rule as the
abundance class.

- 戊午 (*Tsuchinoe-Uma*), 壬辰 (*Mizunoe-Tatsu*).

> Editor's closing note: *"The foregoing is the great sixty-year
> cycle (*dai-shū rokujūnen*, 大周六十年)."*

In these years the year is abundant but of middling quality.

### ◯ 大潮小汐の考 — *Consideration of large and small tides*

The **小汐** (*ko-shio*, small tide) days of each month are the
6th, 7th, 8th and 9th, the 10th, the 21st, 22nd, 23rd, 24th and
25th. All other days are **大潮** (*ōshio*, large tide). The three
days of small tide are *ō-tani* days; great winds rise then. A wind
starts with the flooding tide and quiets at the full tide.

On the fifteenth and the last day of the month, the tide is full
at the sixth (dawn/dusk) bell, and low at the ninth (noon/midnight).

> *"The flooding tide gains 4-*bu* each day, and the ebbing tide
> runs 4-*bu* later each day."*

### ◯ 風吹ふかざるを考うる — *Considering when the wind will and will not blow*

From the day of **庚申** (*Kanoe-Saru*), for eleven days; from the
day of **庚午** (*Kanoe-Uma*); from (blank day) through the day of
**丁丑** (*Hinoto-Ushi*). For the fifteen days covered by those
runs, no wind blows. From the day of **戊寅** (*Tsuchinoe-Tora*),
for seven days more — again, fifteen windless days.

This is why ships are taken out to sea within these windows; if the
wind rises outside them, treat it as one in a hundred years — in
the long run, of no consequence.

## Confidence

**High confidence**: the seven-class structure of the almanac —
暴風 12 / 洪水 5 / 寒冷 3 / 霖雨 2 / 極凶 8 / 豊作 28 / 中豊作 2
(summing to 60, the full sexagenary cycle) — and the per-class
trading rules (e.g., *"at the 210th day, deploy the 三平二乗
半板出染転変 trade"*; *"at the autumn meigetsu, deploy the
三平二乗半板安楽転変 trade"*). The editor's closing note that
the aggregate is a **大周六十年** (*dai-shū rokujū-nen*) is
confirmed.

**Medium confidence**: the individual sexagenary lists within each
class. The year-counts match the class-counts in most cases, but
the OCR artefacts in the **極凶八箇年** list (`丙寅丙寅丙寅丙寅
丙寅` appears to be a stamp-slur) prevent a clean recovery of all
eight years; four of the eight are recoverable. The sexagenary
reading of 辛来 as 辛未 is a standard cursive emendation.

**Lower confidence / interpretive**: the variant-ki glosses
(*mokki uppatsu*, *doki uppatsu*, etc.) paired with each class
are recorded as the OCR presents them; the 五行 / 運気 tradition
they index is a larger Edo-period weather-calendar doctrine that
would reward a separate editorial apparatus. The tide and wind
cycles on page 16 are high-confidence on their sexagenary anchors
(庚申 / 庚午 / 丁丑 / 戊寅) but the day-counts around them (11, 7,
15) are OCR-derived and should be cross-checked against a
standard Edo almanac (*gyokurekishō*) before citation.
