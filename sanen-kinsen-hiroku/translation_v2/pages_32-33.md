# Pages 32–33: Editor's colophon and final blank leaf

## Page 32 — Colophon by 鳴川繁楽 (Narukawa Shigeraku), dated 1851

*[Interpretive. The colophon is written in a late-Edo epistolary
register — allusive, with frequent kana-to-kanji hybrids that the
NDL OCR has handled imperfectly. The rendering below tracks the
apparent argument of the passage rather than a line-for-line
literal reading; the surviving OCR phrasing is shown after in the
original for every paragraph.]*

In the present age, amid things announced and answered, the
reader's eye arrives late, and does not see what lies before the
sorrow: like leaves of writing caught on the slope of seeking —
visible in passing, yet not waited upon. Without that waiting, the
errors that settle onto the rule are many.

If, taking this kind of reading, one enters the market on a
spring-buy, there is the old saying: *"one step aside, a thousand
*li* in divergence."* Failing to stop at that one step, turning
back in hope, one is swallowed into a great mountain-waiting
instead.

Four or five years ago, I began to feel the trouble of this —
people copying and handing about the several books in the tradition
— overlaid as they were with age, substitutions, and editor's
marks. To exalt one variant and call it the original would not be
honest. And so, each one of these readings —

Since I entered this path myself, counting the years that have
passed, I have no confidence that, even in the likeness of *Ushida
Daisuke* — [OCR `牛田大介`; the colophon's author refers to his
predecessor **牛田権三郎** under a variant or honorific
appellation, the reading of which is uncertain] — some errors may
not yet remain.

I fear this. If there are men of discerning eye who can address it
openly, the mountain path may yet open through to the authentic
side. And so I commit it.

> **嘉永四年五月三日** — *Kaei 4, third day of the fifth month*
> [= 2 June 1851]
>
> **鳴川繁楽** — *Narukawa Shigeraku*
>
> **さる** — *saru* ("monkey" — a playful sign-off invoking the
> book's title figure of the three monkeys).
