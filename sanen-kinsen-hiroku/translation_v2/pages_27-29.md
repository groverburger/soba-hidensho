### ◯ 度端一六の商内 — *The six-part limit trade*

From *chūzumi* 20 tawara: when a rise or a fall of more than three
*koku* comes out, always stand **one hundred ryō at the cheap
side** and trade. Whether one thousand or two thousand ryō, treat
the call as a high-side case. A move of more than three *koku*,
rising or falling, happens only once in a thousand — likewise a
"mountain" fall is one in a thousand.

### ◯ 立用商内 — *Standby-application trade*

In the **tatsuyō** trade, regardless of the season, listen always
to the standing rise and fall of rice in the several locales, and
consider carefully before setting out. *At times, level-goods are
mispriced.* The *tatsuyō* is for those who trade in large volume,
and it pays out well: for, when fifty or a hundred thousand ryō of
rice is held long and a rise comes, one *wants* to sell — but the
moment one lays sell, others from the side push in after, and the
price dips six or seven *bu*, sometimes further, *because* the
market follows your move. For that reason the position cannot be
disposed of as one wished. In a *tatsuyō*, by contrast, the heart
is at ease; there is no trouble.

— 7-*bu*, *sell* on that upward ship. At a subsequent 5-*bu* or
7-*bu* fall, *buy* there. **This is called 隠色** (*kakurō*).

The size must be small: one hundred or two hundred ryō, nothing
more. Without certainty, to pile in large is the **手荷の強気**
(*tenimotsu no tsuyoki*, the bullishness of a man bearing a
hand-load) — folly. Otherwise, follow-on chasing (*oi-yobi
oi-ori*) is a perfectly good trade in its own right. This trade is
called **添子すくみ** (*soeko-sukumi*, "catch the attendant
child"). Reading through 随意 (*zui-i*, "as one wills") to
*kakurō* — that is the first frame.

### ◯ 度端極意の巻 — *Chapter of the limit-extremity secret*

The trade called **度端** (*dohan*) takes as its ultimate secret
*to cut desire, and let go* (*yoku o kirite hanatsu*). From the
first impulse the greater-than-greater is the order: this is the
root of rice trading. **If you do not scatter, you cannot lose at
a thousand turns in a single seat.**

## Page 29 — Closing the limit-extremity diagram; opening "the function of the bull"

Take this diagram as the **度端の極意** (*dohan no gokui*).

- The **中央方円** (*chūō hōen*, central square-and-circle) figures
  **天地** (heaven and earth).
- The **四方・十方** (*shihō*, *jippō*, four cardinal and ten
  directions) on the four sides: **東西南北** (east, west, south,
  north).
- The **四隅** (*yonsumi*, four corners) figure **乾坤** (*kenkon*,
  heaven-and-earth) — the 46-day intervals of the year.
- Taken together, these **exhibit the ten tiers** (*jippun*).

The function of the diagram:

- **On the buy side**, the governing principles are drought, storm,
  *tenka-buy-close* (天災・買〆), and their like — arguments
  through all four seasons for the earth-rising, strongly bullish
  person. These arguments are many.
- **On the sell side**, there is no principle at all beyond waiting
  for a buy-close's collapse. For this reason the diagram
  manifestly carries a **一割の強み** (*ichi-koku no tsuyomi*), a
  one-*koku* strength on the bull side.

### ◯ 強気の功 — *The function of the bull*

*[Opens a short catalogue of seasonal weather-and-market
correspondences. It runs onto page 30.]*

When wind and rain rage, in every season ships founder. Both the
Edo and Osaka sea-lanes will have **船間** (*funa-ma*,
ship-intervals, delays). Discernment comes first.

In gale seasons, in every season, treat the matter as grave —
hold it in mind.

From the third or fourth month through to the eighth or ninth,
when wind and rain rage, it is a matter of (damaged) fresh-water
paddies and fields, and of villages at large.

- Drought in the fourth or fifth month: no planting.
- Long rain in those months: damage to wheat; shio-run, nori-run;
  blue-wilt of sprouts.
