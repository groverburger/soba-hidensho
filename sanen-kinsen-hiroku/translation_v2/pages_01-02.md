# Pages 1–2: Covers and library markings

*[Interpretive. Both pages are front-matter leaves and cover boards. The
OCR consists almost entirely of mechanical artifacts, library accession
stamps, and partial title strips. What follows describes what is
legibly present rather than attempting a literary reading.]*

## Page 1 — Outer cover

A cover leaf. The OCR returns a band of noise (romaji fragments that
appear to be scanner calibration artifacts — `"Ar23456Me…"`, `"Allap."`)
together with a small cluster of legible Japanese markings:

- **百廿九日** (*hyaku-nijūku-nichi*) — "129th day." A dating or
  foliation note of uncertain reference.
- **つゆ** (*tsuyu*) — "rainy season" or a personal name; the context
  is not recoverable from the OCR alone.
- **谷村文** (*Tanimura-bun*) — almost certainly the first three
  characters of *Tanimura bunko* (谷村文庫), a collection name also
  stamped on page 2.
- **廿九日** (*nijūku-nichi*) — "29th day."
- **一正三猿金泉秘録金泉秘録全** — the title cartouche. The repeated
  run `金泉秘録 … 金泉秘録全` (*Kinsen Hiroku … Kinsen Hiroku zen*) is
  the cover slip for *Sanen Kinsen Hiroku*, "complete" (*zen*). The
  leading `一正` is best read as an editorial tick rather than part
  of the title.

## Page 2 — Inner title leaf and ownership marks

An inner leaf carrying a secondary title and library provenance.

- **活法秘論首巻** (*Kappō Hiron shukan*) — "Secret Treatise on the
  Living Method, opening volume." This is a collateral title or
  series designation under which the text was bound; *Sanen Kinsen
  Hiroku* follows as the proper title.
- **全** (*zen*) — "complete."
- **校正三猿金泉秘録** (*Kōsei Sanen Kinsen Hiroku*) — "Proofread
  edition of *Sanen Kinsen Hiroku*." The *kōsei* marker signals this
  is the corrected recension by the later editor named in the
  preface (Narukawa Mōnosuke, 鳴川猛之助).
- **91006836** — library accession number.
- **嶋田君寄贈** (*Shimada-kun kizō*) — "donated by Mr. Shimada."
- **谷村文庫** [OCR: `香村攻庫` → likely `谷村文庫` (*Tanimura Bunko*)]
  — the Tanimura Collection stamp.
- A long rubber-stamp band `第一著著著著著…` — the OCR has
  converted a repeated stamp impression into a run of `著` and
  should be read as a single library seal rather than meaningful
  text.
