# Pages 17–20: The nine essentials and the three virtues (智・仁・勇)

## Page 17 — Closing divinations; opening of the nine essentials

### ◯ 入梅三十日 — *Rainy-season entry, thirty days*

The rainy season (*tsuyu*) enters at the start of the fifth month
and lifts in the sixth. Know this in advance.

### ◯ 虹図 — *Rainbow chart*

*"A morning rainbow — read it as 'morning rainbow' in advance. An
evening-sun rainbow is fair weather."*

### ◯ 八専十二日 — *Hassen, twelve days*

The *hassen* period begins on the day of **壬子** (*Mizunoe-Ne*)
and ends on **癸亥** (*Mizunoto-I*) — twelve days — with an eleventh
intercalary day. If *hassen*-entry brings light rain, rain falls
through to *hassen*-close; know it in advance. If it rains again in
between, the twelfth day is called **鳥がへりふる**
(*tori-gaeri furu*, "the bird-return rain").

### ◯ 玄雲目 — *Cloud-reading*

*"A cloud rising with red mixed in — read it as wind. White cloud
or black cloud — read it as rain."*

### ◯ 一部極意九躰の歌 — *Nine-body song of the singular secret*

[OCR: `一部極意九躰ノ歎` → likely `九躰ノ歌`.]

A sequence of nine headings, each with a single defining couplet.
The first five, given on this page:

**1. 無極** (*mukyoku*) — *the Unbounded*:
> *"The principle of high-and-low is the empty principle — unseen
> by the eye, without shadow, without form — no-thing is its body."*

**2. 太極** (*taikyoku*) — *the Great Ultimate*:
> *"Between the 'is' and the 'is-not' there stands a principle
> without name — the principle of the rice-window only."* [OCR
> partly illegible at `鯉と非とのなかに…理州の理…`; the rendering
> captures what is recoverable.]

**3. 陰食陽** (*in shoku yō*) — *yin consuming yang*:
> *"When every one of the ten thousand is bearish, the rice holds
> within itself the principle that must rise."*

**4. 陽食陰** (*yō shoku in*) — *yang consuming yin*:
> *"When every one of the thousand is bullish, the rice holds
> within itself the principle that must sink."*

**5. 高安大法** (*kōan taihō*) — *the great law of high and low*:
> *"The great law: at the autumn meigetsu the high peak; at the
> close of the fifth month the low hollow."*

**6. 天ノ性** (*ten no shō*) — *the nature of heaven*:
> *"In both high-and-low, in the fifth month the ten-thousand-*bu*
> natural profit appears at its appointed time. Know it in

**7. 商定式** (*shōjōshiki*) — *the fixed method of trading*:
> *"High or low: take every 5-*bu* / 1-*koku* as the measure;
> never forget two-*wari*, three-*koku* as the silver thread."*
> [OCR dense; rendering is approximate.]

**8. 平来定式** (*heirai jōshiki*) — *fixed method of level-riding*:
> *"Buying and selling is levelled at 5-*bu* highs and lows;
> similarly at 5-*bu* the high."* (Margin note: *this chapter given

**9. 売買定式** (*baibai jōshiki*) — *fixed method of buying and

> *"The buying time is the meigetsu of Tango (端午名月, the

> minazuki and fumizuki."*

### ◯ 智仁勇三徳 四十八首 — *The three virtues, in forty-eight verses*

The verses that follow are divided into three blocks — **智**
(wisdom), **仁** (benevolence), **勇** (courage). An opening
editorial gloss frames them:

> *"Proofreader's note: the song of knowing — to seek is the
> order-profit (順徳). What is heard with good intent leaves a
> track."*

### ◯ 知十首 — *Ten verses of 智 (knowing)*

> *"Proofreader says: 智 means *to seek* — the order-profit, the
> order-profit."* [The gloss is repeated verbatim in the OCR.]

- When a trade has become an order-trade, to see the hundred
  thousand ryō's resolution in a single move — that is the secret
  of knowing.
- Without rushing, without answering, simply waiting to face what
  comes — the universal principle unabandoned — that is the
  virtue of wisdom.
- To wait is **仁** (*jin*); to face in is — *[gap]*; knowing the
  target of the selling-principle — that is the marvellous art of
  **半橋商内** (*hankyō shōnai*). [OCR `半橋` uncertain; elsewhere
  the book uses 半板; the two may stand for the same configuration.]
- **天元商内** (*ten'gen shōnai*): even if it does not ripen for
  three years, know that for *Ranzai* [a Chinese reference,
  uncertain from OCR] it is the fundamental trading verse.
- In high-and-low, 5-*bu* and 1-*koku* as the mulberry-wood
  measure is good; to past-the-*chūzumi* bury is foolish. [OCR
  `桑` and `葬` read literally; both recur as trading figures.]
- In high-and-low, for a long foot, **phlegm** (*tan*, 痰) is good;
  for the pheasant's mouth-up, the mulberry is good. [OCR:
  `痰` literal; almost certainly an idiom or a marginal
  figure-name in this book. Treat reading as uncertain.]
- When the wind from the middle brings on the latter-roasting
  season (*aburi no shun*), with the gold-king alive — that is
  the fly-rise.
- When the *minazuki* rice's break shows, a spring-field evening
  brings the fly-fall.
- When fifth-month rice has weak sentiment and yet the price
  rises, know it as the season of the fly-rise trade.
- If the fly-come time has already come but has not yet flown —
  cast the principle into the water and follow the rice.

## Page 19 — 仁 (benevolence), twenty-eight verses

### ◯ 二十八首 — *Twenty-eight verses of 仁*

Editor's gloss: *"仁 means four — *waiting for the season* — and
*not scattering*."* [OCR: `仁ハ四ナリ時節午待ナリイナカスナリ`;
`イナカス` read as `否散す`.]

- Not rushing the buy, not rushing the sell — waiting until the
  reserves ride the market — that itself is 仁.
- To hurry in trading brings no profit. Buy 5-*bu* cheap, sell
  5-*bu* high.
- Rush and lose; rush harder and lose more. Stop, take a different
  hand. Look again.
- At the break of the rice of the *i* year (亥年の米のきれめ),
  wait out the sound; *selling* after waiting is 仁's virtue.
- Merely rushing raises a hundred tawara and drops a hundred
  tawara — this is the common pattern.
- *The secret of the bull side is not to rush the buy* — whenever
  enough comes up to show itself, buy then.
- *The secret of the bear side is not to rush the sell* — whenever
  enough is accumulated, sell then.
- A cheap price that comes only once in two or three years: wait
  without rushing — *that* is the great cunning.
- Wait too for the season when the level-trade is unnecessary: to
  hurry into it is to step on the sparrow's claw.
- Even a falling principle will not fall before its time; to
  hurry the buy is great folly.
- Even a rising principle will not rise before its time; there is
  nothing to regret in selling the light way.
- Because of hurrying, one sells cheap and buys high — and
  straight away steps on the sparrow's claw.
- When the sense arrives that the price should rise, the ease is
  one's nature — know it as *tensei* (天性).
- When the price falls and falls into the bear, the bear
  exhausts itself and the ease is again one's nature — know it
  as *tensei*.
- On the cheap day of the wind-less 220th day, take the
  half-rise and sow.
  [OCR: `定式として半はうけてころ 万人別あきれはてたる直がおれ候そそれ
  前高くりの毘なりけり`; rendering captures the gist.]
- On rain-and-wind days, watch and sell; on fair days, watch and
  buy.
- The source-spring of the order-profit of 智 lies in nothing else
  — its source-spring, in the end, lies in 仁.

## Page 20 — 勇 (courage) verses

- The jewel principle (玉ふ理): *sell the many and buy the cheap
  — the great secret of rice trading.*
- A trade that faces a three-*koku* high-and-low is water pouring
  like gold to the king.
- *Even a three-*wari* high-or-low need not trouble you — face it
  squarely.* (Margin: *Tenmei recension.*)
- When a high-or-low of two to three *koku* appears, know it is
  the time to face the rice.
- A losing trade: do not give over; level it off.
- For both *oke* trading and level trading: all *are* the bait on
  the hook — dangle it, then pull it up.
- At a three-*wari* high-or-low, virtue *tenpen*'s, and loss
  levels — that is the secret.
- If each and every one of the ten thousand is bullish, play the
  fool and sell.
- *A great rice collapse, a great crisis — do not be alarmed.
  Buy.* (Margin: *Tenmei recension.*)
- If mountain and field alike look only toward the bear — play
  the idiot and buy.
- When no judgment nor deliberation needs be asked of you, there
  is the rice-collapse that is yours to catch.
- At a rice-collapse after a bought-*rakujō* of weak-hollow,
  buy the pile.
- At a rice-collapse after a sold-*rakujō* of high-hollow, sell
  the pile.
- In a rich-year collapse of rice — buy.
  *In a lean-year standing-high — sell.*
  [OCR: `豊卑のくずるゝ米は買みよし / 尚きをまはて立耳はられど
  ころをまはてうるべし`.]
- On a day when rice is cheap and sentiment weak — buy. On a day
  when it is high and sentiment strong — that is the bottom (or
  peak); sell.
- When one thousand out of one thousand are bearish, counter and
  buy; when ten thousand are strong, counter and sell.
- Spring: face a two-*koku* high-or-low. Summer: face a
  three-*koku* high-or-low. *Face, face, face.*
- **三俵鞘直照可レ逆** [marginal rubric, Tenmei recension]: at a
  three-*tawara* saya, the direct reading is *to counter*.
  Editor's gloss: *"Because it follows that the three-fold is a
  *koku*-and-a-half, one takes this as law."*
- At high-or-low, follow the hands of others; at an hour-saya of
  one thing, sow the *hako* seed. *[The couplet runs across lines
  27 of page 20 into line 1 of page 21; the two halves are
  preserved together here.]*
