## Page 5 — Preface signature and opening of the body

The tail of the preface runs onto this page before the body begins.

*[…continuing from page 4.]* When old rice is scarce and a high
price carries over to the new rice, that is a year of **空腹下り**
(*kūfuku-sagari*), *empty-belly fall*. In the sixth, seventh, and
eighth months bullishness may yet appear, but the year is truly one

The fixed method of trading is then **逆・平・順乗** (*gyaku / hei /
junjō*): at the reverse, buy level; at *junjō* (順乗), make the
upward principle out clearly and buy into the come.

The family transmissions I have collected here — the

**家伝高安鑑** (*kaden kōan-kagami*), the **三十八来商内**

**万歳運気豊凶録** (*banzai unki hōkyō-roku*), and the rest — when

the house's secret store. Keep it secret; keep it secret.

### ◯ 順来変来十二平商内 — *Junrai-henrai twelve-level trading* (天明本 — from the Tenmei recension)

Twelve verses grouped into four sets of six: two sets of six for
*junrai* (年々 of abundant old rice), and two sets of six for
*henrai* (years of scarce old rice).

**古米おほき豊年の高安順来六句** — *Six junrai verses on high and low
in a rich year with abundant old rice*:

- Autumn cheap; empty-belly rise of a *koku*-and-a-half; in *shimotsuki*
  (11th month) a fall; at year-end a rise.
- Spring divided; a fall in the fourth and fifth months; rice in
  *minazuki* (6th) and *fumizuki* (7th) falls further.
  *[Interpretive. OCR for this line is fragmentary (`春時わり四五月下
  り水無月と文月の米下るものなり`); the sense is clear but the exact
  phrasing is uncertain.]*

**順乗六句商内** — *Six verses of junjō trading*:

- Autumn, buy; at empty-belly rise, sell at *tenpen* (転変); at
  *kichigetsu* (吉月) again *tenpen*; at year-end, *anraku* (安楽).
- Spring *tenpen*; fourth–fifth month, buy; at the end of the fifth
  month plant the seed [for] *hako* (はこ); sixth, seventh, eighth
  months, the harvest.
  *[Interpretive. OCR `春転変四五月は買五月寸ゑはこの種土け六七八の
  掌`.]*

**古米すくなき凶年の高安変来六句** [OCR: `古米すくなき五年の高安変衰
大句` → likely `古米すくなき凶年の高安変来六句`] — *Six henrai verses
on high and low in a year of scarce old rice*:

- Autumn high; empty-belly fall of a *koku*-and-a-half; *shimotsuki*
  cheap; year-end also does not rise.

### ◯ 家伝四季高安鑑 第二 — *House-transmitted four-season high-low reckoning, the second* (天明本)

Paired verses, *junrai* and *henrai*, for each season.

**順来秋冬の高下を考ふる歌**:
When old rice is abundant and the year seen as bountiful, know that
cheap rice is an empty-belly-rise year.

**変来秋冬の高下を考ふる歌**:
When old rice is scarce and bullishness begins to show, know that
high rice is an empty-belly-fall year.

**順来変来春の高下の考へ歌**:
Seen as a blue-rise, with rice bought up — such rice will not easily
turn cheap.
Seen as a spring-fall, with the cheap winter price straightforward —
even through spring, the rice will not easily stay cheap.

**三月・春の高下** — *Third-month, spring highs and lows*:

In the third month, if the great fall has not come through — on a
high day, sell, on a sell-through day switch.

At *suwari* (居り) — **三平二乗半板商内** (*sanpei nijō hanban
shōnai*), three-level / two-*jō* / half-*ban* trading: on both high
and low, level-riding is forbidden.
At *suwari*: sell at *tenpen*, buy at *tenpen*; the short-line verse
that warns against letting virtue (profit) escape.

**順来変来五月高下の考へ歌**:
A junjō year has, without fail, a fifth-month rise; a fifth-month
fall makes the year a henrai year.

The great law — at autumn *meigetsu* (秋名月), the high peak; at the
end of the fifth month, the low hollow. *[OCR: `大法が秋名月かやす峠
五月下旬がこかとらげなり`; the paired terms here (`たかとらげ`,
`こかとらげ`) appear throughout the text as the peak/hollow pair.]*

If fifth-month rice has weak public sentiment and yet the price
climbs, for children and grandchildren: **selling is forbidden.**

## Page 7 — The thirty-eight-verse trading song

**順来夏の高下を考ふる歌**:
If the level rises and from the next phase the fifth month's rice is
high, it is a year in which *minazuki* and *fumizuki* fall.

**変来夏の高下を考ふる歌**:
If the level opens from spring and the fifth month yields, it is a
year in which *minazuki* and *fumizuki* rise.

### ◯ 三十八首 — *Thirty-eight verses* (三十八来商内)

A sequence of thirty-eight trading verses. The OCR runs the sequence
together without clear numeric divisions and several lines are
partly corrupt; what follows is the recoverable reading, verse by
verse.

- In a *junjō* year, the hollow-belly rise trails behind: at autumn
  *meigetsu*, sow the buying seed.
- In a *henrai* year, the hollow-belly fall trails behind: at autumn
  *meigetsu*, sow the selling seed.
- When a lower-*saya* becomes an upper-*saya*, the *saya*-change
  trails a rise behind it; switch to **徳乗り** (*toku-nori*,
  profit-riding).
- When an upper-*saya* becomes a lower-*saya*, the *saya*-change
  trails a fall behind it; switch to *toku-nori*.
- From the end of the fifth month, sow the selling seed.
  *[Interpretive. A preceding line is illegible (`□□□□…`); the
  verse above restores the general cadence of the surrounding
  couplets but not the specific missing wording.]*
- The **天性の利** (*tensei no ri*) — the natural profit — appears in
  *minazuki*. Whether high or low, follow the rice.
- If rice that ought to fall rises out of the hollow, switch to
  *toku-nori* to the extent of the profit that remains.
- At the end of the fifth month, if high, rush the **はこ** (*hako*)
  seed; in the sixth and seventh months, sell with *toku-nori*.
- At the end of the fifth month, if low, sow the **子山**
  (*ko-yama*, "small mountain") seed; in the sixth and seventh
  months, switch to *toku-nori*.
- At a rice-miss — after a bought *落城* (*rakujō*, "fall of the
  castle") and a sudden fly-fall — narrow the eye and sow the
  buying seed.
- At a rice-drought — after a sold *rakujō* and a sudden fly-fall —
  play the fool and sow the *hako* seed.
- After flood and great wind bring on a fly-rise — play the idiot
  and sow the *hako* seed.
- If fifth-month rice has weak sentiment yet the price rises, in
  late fourth month, the *ko-yama* seeds are in order.
- When bullishness shows itself in people's voices, that is the
  seed for rice to fall.
- When calamity-change shows, all turn bullish without discretion;
  sow the selling seed.
- In a year when bearishness shows in the world, the principle is
  that rice must rise.

- The one who is always bearish, not knowing gain or loss — a great

### ◯ 十五禁制 — *The fifteen prohibitions*

*[A marginal `十五首` and `十四` confirm the enumeration. What
follows runs onto page 9.]*

Without reserves ready at hand, whatever the moment, gain and loss
both even out to nothing — **trading is forbidden.**
