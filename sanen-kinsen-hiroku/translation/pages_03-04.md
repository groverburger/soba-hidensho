author, editor, publisher — followed immediately by the opening of the
preface. The preface continues across page 4 and closes with the
attribution to Ushida Gonzaburō.

## Page 3 — Left side: Preface opening

### 三猿金泉録序 — *Preface to the Three Monkeys Gold Spring Record*

The preface is written in Chinese-derived prose with Japanese reading
marks (*kundoku*), in a more formal hand than the body of the text.
Its opening passage places the rice market inside the Neo-Confucian
cosmology of the Supreme Ultimate and the alternation of yin and yang.

> **太極動きて陽を生じ、動くこと極りて静なり。**
>
> **静にして陰を生ず。静なること極りて又動く。**
>
> *The Supreme Ultimate (太極, Taikyoku) moves and begets Yang; its
> motion reaches its limit and becomes still. In stillness it begets
> Yin; stillness reaches its limit and moves again.*

*[OCR: 「またらごく」 is mojibake for 「又動く」; the cursive 動 was
miscut into two glyphs.]*

The preface continues:

> 太極陰陽と天地万物の始まり、一動一静あるに其の根となる。
>
> 米の高下も天地陰陽の廻るがごとく、強気の功あらはれて甚だ高く
> 上る理極れば、其の中に弱気の理を含む。弱気の功あらはれて甚だ
> 安らになれば、其の中に強気の理を含む。

The rise and fall of rice — like the circuit of Heaven and Earth, and
of Yin and Yang — alternates between **strong-spirited force** (強気,
*kyōki*) and **weak-spirited force** (弱気, *jakki*). Each contains
the seed of its opposite: when the strong force has driven prices to
their ceiling, the weak force is already present within it; when the
weak force has driven them to their floor, the strong force is
likewise already present.

> 万人気弱き時は米上るべきの理なり。諸人気強き時は米下るべきの
> 種なり。是れみな天性理外の理なり。

*When the crowd's spirit is weak, it is the principle of rice's
rising. When the crowd's spirit is strong, it is the seed of rice's
falling. All of this is the principle beyond ordinary nature*
(**天性理外の理**, *tensei rigai no ri*).

> 予、壮年の頃より米商に心を……

*[Transition to page 4 mid-sentence: "From my years of vigor, I set
my heart upon rice trading …"]*

## Page 4 — Continuation of the preface

The preface continues without paragraph break across the fold:

> 寄せ、昼夜工夫をめぐらし、六十年来月日を送りて、漸々米強弱の
> 悟りを開きて、米商の定法を起こて一巻の秘書を作り、号けて
> **三猿金泉録**といふ。

*For sixty years I have cast my mind upon this craft, devising by day
and by night, and have at length opened up the insight into rice's
strong and weak forces. I have established the settled method of rice
trading, and have compiled a single volume of secret writings, giving
it the title* **Three Monkeys Gold Spring Record**.

The claim of **six decades of study** is a weighty one. It places the
book as the summation of the author's working life — not a young
trader's manual but a last testament.

### The shape of the rice grain

> 米穀の形象は、中まるく上下尖る。まるは陽、尖りは陰なり。

*The form of the rice grain: rounded in the middle, pointed at top
and bottom. The round is Yang; the pointed is Yin.*

This is the foundational image of the whole book — the grain itself
becomes the emblem for the candle/spike pattern of a market print.
The same figure recurs in the *Record of Spike Patterns* (穂足の録)
later in the volume.

### The four classes, and the three monkeys

> 天地陰陽の気をうけ、四民工商をやしなふ天下第一の宝なり。

*Receiving the breath of Heaven, Earth, Yin and Yang, [rice]
nourishes the four classes — farmers, artisans, merchants — and is
the first treasure of all under Heaven.*

*[OCR: 「士略」 mid-column is almost certainly a fragment of 士農,
the "warrior" and "farmer" that open the standard four-classes
enumeration (士農工商, *shi-nō-kō-shō*); the text appears to have been
set slightly off column.]*

> 三猿とは、見猿・聞猿・言猿の三なり。

*The Three Monkeys are: the See-Monkey, the Hear-Monkey, and the
Speak-Monkey.*

Then, three parallel admonitions built on the famous triad:

> 眼に強気を見て、心に強気の淵に沈むことなかれ。只だ心に含みて
> 売るべし。
>
> 耳に弱気を聞きて、心に弱気の淵に沈むことなかれ。只だ心に含み
> て買ふべし。
>
> 強気・弱気を見聞とも、人に語ることなかれ。言は人の心を迷はす。
> 是れ三猿の秘密なり。

*When the eye perceives strong-spiritedness, do not let the heart
sink into the abyss of that strength. Hold it only within the heart,
and* sell.

*When the ear hears weak-spiritedness, do not let the heart sink into
the abyss of that weakness. Hold it only within the heart, and* buy.

*Whether you see or hear the strong or the weak, do not speak of it
to others. Words lead hearts astray. This is the secret of the three
monkeys.*

*[OCR: 「強変 / 弱変」 throughout this passage → 強気 / 弱気. The
cursive 気 is a known confusion with 変; the surrounding discussion
of 強気 / 弱気 (above and through the rest of the book) fixes the
reading.]*

> 金泉録とは、此の書の号なり。

*"Gold Spring Record" — this is the style of this book.*

### On the principle of highs and lows

> 高安の理は空理にて、眼に見えず、かげも形も無き物が躰なり。

*The principle of highs and lows is an* empty principle (**空理**,
*kūri*) — *not visible to the eye; a thing whose very body is without
shade or form.*

The preface then contrasts two readings of the word 空理 (*kūri*):

> 上の句の心を考ふるに、法をもて上るともいつ下るとも一定まで
> さざるが空理なり。空理と見れば千年に一度も売り買ひする時節
> となし。
>
> また下の句の心を考ふるに、かげも形も無き物が体とあり、体あれ
> ば定式あり、定式あれば売買あり。

*Reading the first line's sense: a principle by which neither the
rise nor the fall is ever fixed — this is an empty principle. If we
take it only as empty, there is no hour in a thousand years to buy
or sell.*

*Reading the second line's sense: the formless thing does have a
body; if there is a body, there is a* fixed form (定式, *jōshiki*);
*if there is a fixed form, there is a trade.*

This is the book's justification for its own project: the market's
motion is ultimately empty, but it has a discernible form, and from
the form one can derive a method.

### Precedent in the three teachings

> 仏道の定式は五戒、儒道の定式は五常、神道の定式は智仁勇の三徳。
> みなそれぞれ定式あり。

*The fixed form of Buddhism is the Five Precepts. The fixed form of
Confucianism is the Five Constants. The fixed form of Shinto is the
Three Virtues of Wisdom, Benevolence and Courage. Each has its own
fixed form.*

By placing rice trading alongside the disciplines of the three
teachings, the preface gives the enterprise a deliberate moral and
intellectual footing.

### Closing and transition to the main text

> 〔…〕の定式、古米おほく安き直段を新米へうつしたるの歳は、
> 〔…〕腹上り有る年なり。是を**順来**の年といふ。

*In years where old rice is plentiful and its low price transfers to
the new rice, there is a rising force of markets. This is called a*
**favorable year** (**順来**, *junrai*). *[The conjunctive particle
is garbled in the OCR.]*

The explanation of its paired opposite — the *adverse year* (**逢来**,
*airai*) — continues at the head of page 5.
