# Pages 17–20: Weather Signs, the Nine-Body Song, and the Three Virtues

These four pages complete the weather-and-calendar almanac of pages
14–16 with a group of folk-signs (rainy season, rainbows, clouds,
the *hassen* interval), then present two of the book's central
theoretical constructions: the **Nine-Body Song** (九躰ノ歌) — a
Neo-Confucian nesting of the cosmological terms *wuji* / *taiji* /
*yin-containing-yang* / *yang-containing-yin* onto the rice market
— and the book's largest enumerated section, the **Forty-Eight
Verses of the Three Virtues** (智仁勇三徳, *Chi-Jin-Yū sanbukken*),
organized as Wisdom (*chi*), Benevolence (*jin*), and Courage (*yū*)
totaling forty-eight verses.

---

## Page 17 — Weather signs and the Nine-Body Song

### ◯ On the Entry of the Rainy Season, Thirty Days (入梅三十日)

> 梅は五月始めの書〔午〕にいり、六月〔中〕にあくとしるべし。

*Know that the rainy season (*tsuyu*) enters at the horse-day
beginning the fifth month and opens out at the middle of the sixth.*

### ◯ On Rainbows (虹図)

> 朝にじる朝にじりと兼ねてしれ。夕日の虹は日和なりけり。

*A morning rainbow — know in advance as a morning-rain sign.
The rainbow at sunset, on the other hand, is fair weather.*

### ◯ The Hassen Twelve Days (八専十二日)

The *hassen* is a traditional twelve-day interval of the sexagenary
calendar, running from the *Mizunoe-Ne* (壬子) day through the
*Mizunoto-I* (癸亥) day. The almanac assigns it a specific rain
sign:

> 八専のいるゝに小雨なりいたせば、あくまで降ると兼ねて知るべし。

*If light rain sets in at the beginning of the hassen, know in
advance that it will keep raining until the end.*

> あいたるにまた降〔り〕いたせば、十二日は鳥がへりふるといふ舟に。

*If it begins again after a pause, the twelve days — in the saying
— pass in an unceasing return-rain that follows the boats.*

### ◯ On the Standing Cloud (玄雲目)

> 立雲に赤のまじりは風としれ。白き・黒きは雨としるべし。

*A standing cloud mingled with red — know it for wind. White or
black — know it for rain.*

### ◯ Part One: The Secret — Song of Nine Bodies (一部極意　九躰ノ歌)

This is the book's most concentrated theoretical passage: a verse-
cycle in which the rice market is set against each of the nested
Neo-Confucian cosmological bodies in turn, from the undifferentiated
**Limitless** (無極, *Mukyoku* / *Wuji*) down through the **Supreme
Ultimate** (太極, *Taikyoku* / *Taiji*) and its yin-yang containments.

#### 無極 — *The Limitless*

> 高安の理は空理にて眼に見えず、かげも〔形〕者もなき物が体。

*The principle of highs and lows is an empty principle (*kūri*) —
invisible to the eye. A thing whose very body is without shadow and
without form.*

#### 太極 — *The Supreme Ultimate*

> 〔是〕と非とのなかにも無き〔ノ〕理、米の窓のみなりとゝ。

*The principle-without-principle that dwells even in the midst of
right and wrong — only the rice-market's window.*

*[OCR: 「鯉と非とのなかにこも無る理州の理」 is noisy; the opening
character is almost certainly 是 ("right"), paired with 非 ("wrong")
in the standard kanbun binome; and the repeated 理 is tangled with
its own dō-phonetic echo in the cursive.]*

#### 陰含陽 — *Yin contains Yang*

> 万人が万人ながら弱気なら、〔升〕のぼるべき理をふくむ米なり。

*When ten-thousand of ten-thousand are all weak-spirited — rice
contains, within itself, the very principle by which it ought to
rise.*

#### 陽含陰 — *Yang contains Yin*

> 千人が千人ながら強気なら、〔く〕ぐるべき理を〔ふ〕くむ米なり。

*When a thousand of a thousand are all strong-spirited — rice
contains, within itself, the very principle by which it ought to
plunge.*

#### 高安大法 — *The Great Law of Highs and Lows*

> 大洪〔水〕が秋名月、前半〔月〕峠、五月下旬が〔高と安〕ゆ〔ら〕げなり。

*The great flood falls upon the autumn harvest moon; the peak at
mid-month; the end of the fifth month — these are the swayings of
high and low.*

## Page 18 — Four definitions, then the opening of the Three Virtues

The page opens with four short headed rules — each a "fixed form"
(定式) of a specific relation. These bridge the theoretical verse-
cycle of page 17 into the enumerated Three-Virtues sequence.

### 天ノ性 — *The nature of heaven*

> 宣下とも、五月〔々〕分、天性の利のなる時と兼て知るべし。

*By imperial edict or otherwise — know in advance that the division
of the fifth month is the time at which natural profit arises.*

### 商定式 — *The fixed form of trading*

> 〔間〕下とも、五分・一刻に随ひて、二割・三刻〔を〕糸銀と忘れ。

*By edict or otherwise — follow the five-*bu* one-step, and forget
the twenty-percent three-step as only silver-string.*

### 平来定式 — *The fixed form of the ordinary run*

*(Annotation: 「〔普〕通本、此一章〔を〕ナトシタリ」 — "in the common
recension, this one article is unrecorded."*)

> 売買は五分高下にて平すべし。或〔る〕も同じく五分・高くり也。

*Buying and selling should be balanced at a five-*bu* swing. Another
version reads the same way: five-*bu* on the high side.*

### 売買定式 — *The fixed form of buying and selling*

> 買〔ふ〕時は、端午・名月、米〔の〕ざよりはこは、水無月・文月として知れ。

*Know the buying times as the* Dragon-Boat Festival *and the harvest
moon, with the seasonal anchor at Minazuki (sixth month) and Fumizuki
(seventh).*

### ◯ The Three Virtues — Wisdom, Benevolence, Courage (智仁勇三徳)

**四十八首** — *Forty-eight verses* — organized into three ranks
after the classical Confucian triad of virtues. In this edition the
running count assigns:

- **智** (*Chi*) — *Wisdom* — **ten verses** (智十首).
- **仁** (*Jin*) — *Benevolence* — **twenty-eight verses**
  (page 19 header).
- **勇** (*Yū*) — *Courage* — ten verses (closing at page 20).

Red-ink running number on the manuscript: **第十五** — *Article
Fifteen* — binds this section into the larger enumerated count of
the book.

#### 智十首 — *Wisdom: Ten Verses*

*Editorial gloss at the head of the section: "The editor notes:
wisdom is a seeking; it is the favorable virtue; it is the trace
of what has been heard in the goodness of the heart." (Paraphrased
from an OCR-fragmentary annotation.)*

> **1.** 順商内となりたらば、万両にも一手に〔葬〕事〔を〕知るの秘密なり。

*Once you have become a favorable-trader, you know — in a single
stroke, even over ten-thousand ryō — the secret of the one who lays
matters to rest.*

> **2.** 多きせば、せかず・答がず、まつ〔は〕に向かへば、普理〔乖〕は智の徳。

*In abundance — neither rush nor reply, face the pine-and-snake,
and the departure from common reason is the virtue of wisdom.*

*[The phrase 「まつはに向かへば」 uses a figurative image
(pine-and-snake) that I have not been able to anchor in the standard
commentarial repertoire; the rendering above preserves the image
without forcing it to a more general paraphrase.]*

> **3.** 一、待つは仁、向かふは売〔の〕理のまと知る、半橋商内の妙術としれ。

*To wait is benevolence. To face the market is to know the target
of the sell-principle — know it as the wondrous art of the
half-bridge trade.*

> **4.** 一、天元商力〔は〕三年もみなるとい、蘭齋、はたがしき本商〔句〕としれ。

*The celestial-origin trading-force may play out even over three
years, says Ransai — know this as the prime-trade epigraph of the
flag-master.*

*[「蘭齋」 here may be a named pen-name, conceivably an earlier
commentator whose gloss was preserved; the identification is not
recoverable from the OCR alone.]*

> **5.** ◯ 宣下とも、五分・一刻は〔桑〕がよし。中墨すぎて〔葬〕は馬鹿なり。

*By edict or otherwise, at the five-*bu* one-step, the mulberry is
good; once you are past the ink-midline, burial is the fool's
course.*

> **6.** 一、〔天〕下とも、長き足には〔桑〕がよし。雉子口〔上〕には桑〔去〕るがよし。

*In all lands alike, when the run is long, the mulberry-stance is
good; when it is only at the pheasant's mouth, one should leave the
mulberry behind.*

> **7.** ◯ 社風・中から後あぶりの旬〔き〕来れば、金の王〔蛇〕きでは飛上りなり。

*When the temple-wind from mid-month brings on the later parching
season, on the gold-king-serpent day, there is the spike upward.*

> **8.** ◯ 水無月の米のきれめの音なぐれ、春田時〔或〕は夕〔に〕飛さが〔る〕なり。

*The echo of the cut-edge of Minazuki rice — at spring-field time,
or in the evening, is a plunge downward.*

> **9.** ◯ 五月米、人気弱く、所尊〔く〕上る — 飛上り商内の旬としるべし。

*Fifth-month rice, when sentiment is weak and its station honored
even as it climbs — know this as the season of spike-upward trading.*

> **10.** ◯ あぶる〔梅〕時、いさだねど等ぶるまじ、理を水にやげて米に随へ。

*Even at the parching rainy season, do not clumsily attempt to spike.
Yield your reasoning to water, and follow the rice.*

## Page 19 — Benevolence: Twenty-Eight Verses

The **仁** (*Jin* — Benevolence) verses open the page. The manuscript
's red-ink header — 「二十八首」 — counts twenty-eight of the
forty-eight Three-Virtue verses to this rank, along with an
editorial note whose surviving reading is **「仁ハ四ナリ、時節午待
ナリ、イナカスナリ」** — *"benevolence is the fourth; it is the
awaiting of the horse-hour; it is* inaka-su *(the pulling-back-to-
countryside)."*

#### 仁廿八首 — *Benevolence: Twenty-Eight Verses*

> **1.** 売買を急がず答がず、〔待〕と〔得〕の〔乘〕まで待つも仁なり。

*Neither rushing the trade nor answering to impatience — to wait
until the riding-together of waiting and gaining — this too is
benevolence.*

> **2.** 只だ、せくるせく商力に徳はなし。五分安を買ひ、五分高を売れ。

*Simply: in the urging-urgent trading-force there is no profit.
Buy the five-*bu* cheap; sell the five-*bu* high.*

> **3.** 売買を急げば急〔れ〕ほど損をする。とんと〔止〕〔め〕で、手をかへて見よ。

*The more you hurry a trade, the more you lose. Simply stop; change
hands and take another look.*

> **4.** 一、〔亥〕年の米のきれめの音などれ、待ちて売るのが仁の徳なり。

*In a pig-year's cut-edge of rice, wait for the report of the sound
before selling — this is the virtue of benevolence.*

> **5.** たゞ、せら〔に〕百俵あげて、百俵さげたためしがおほき事なり.

*Many are the instances in which a hundred *tawara* raised is, plainly,
a hundred *tawara* brought down.*

> **6.** 買せきをせぬが強気の秘密なり。筋でも出きるを〔聞〕いて買ふ。

*Not-rushing-the-buy is the secret of the strong-spirited. Buy on
hearing the pattern emerge.*

> **7.** 売せきをせぬが弱気の秘密なり。いつでも〔有〕もるを待ちて売る。

*Not-rushing-the-sell is the secret of the weak-spirited. In all
seasons, wait for the ripening and sell.*

> **8.** 三年にも二年にもいらぬ安直段、急がずに待つが大込み手なり.

*The cheap prices that are not needed in three years or two — the
big-weight hand is the one that waits without rushing.*

> **9.** 一、平もいらぬ時節を待つがよし。こかせと〔い〕うえに、〔ぶ〕〔ら〕〔ぶ〕〔ら〕すを踏む。

*Wait out even a season in which the flat is not needed. Say
"shake-it-out," and meanwhile tread upon a slow-walk.*

> **10.** 下〔落〕の理にも、時〔が〕至られば下はまじ。買ぜきするは大たわけなり.

*Even in the principle of decline, if the time has come, it will
not go lower. To rush the buy is a great foolishness.*

> **11.** 一、〔上〕〔昇〕の理にも、時節が来ねば上らぬぞ。世を〔置〕きて買をして悔むまじきぞ.

*Even in the principle of the rise, if the season has not come, it
does not rise. Do not buy to the world's timing and have to regret it.*

> **12.** 急くが故に安きを売りて、あたまから〔掛〕けをかけ、〔ぶ〕〔ら〕〔ぶ〕〔ら〕すを踏。

*Because you are rushing, you sell into cheapness, pile the wager
on from the head, and end up walking the slow-walk.*

> **13.** 上るべき気が尽きぬれば、おのづから下るところが天性としれ.

*When the force that ought to rise is spent, the place where it
descends of itself — know that as the nature of heaven.*

> **14.** 一、下〔る〕ほど下〔くだ〕れは、弱気の気もつきて、上〔昇〕〔る〕ところが天性としれ.

*When it falls as far as it will fall, the very force of the
weak-spirited is spent — and the place where it rises is the nature
of heaven.*

> **15.** ◯ 風吹かぬ二百廿日の安直段、定式として半〔板〕を受けて〔行〕.

*The cheap price of the windless two-hundred-twentieth day — take
it as the fixed form, receive it with the half-plank, and go.*

> **16.** 万人〔皆〕別にあきれ果てたる直段が〔あ〕れ〔ば〕、それ前高くりの〔遠〕なりけり.

*When a price exists such that the ten-thousand to a man have given
up in weariness — that is the distant edge of the previous high-run.*

> **17.** ◯ 雨風の日をま〔ち〕受けて米は売り、日和をまして米は買べし.

*Wait for a day of rain and wind and sell rice; gain clear weather
and buy rice.*

> **18.** 順徳の智の〔源〕〔も〕とは、遂〔に〕小〔の〕第、その源とこと、まつ仁にあり.

*The root source of the favorable-virtue wisdom, in the end, is the
small progression — and at that source, too, benevolence stands in
first place.*

*[Verses 19–28 of the Benevolence rank continue onto page 20.]*

## Page 20 — Benevolence continued; opening of Courage

The upper portion of the page carries the final ten verses of
Benevolence and then crosses into **勇** (*Yū*) — Courage — which
the verse-count implies is a ten-verse rank balancing Wisdom.

> **19.** 玉〔なる〕理は、多きを売て安きをかふ、米商内の大秘密なり.

*The principle of the jewel: sell into plenty, buy into cheapness —
this is the great secret of rice trading.*

> **20.** 一、三刻の高下に向ふ商内は、かねの王〔出〕でる、〔湧く〕いづみとはしと.

*A trade that engages a three-step high-low: it is where the king
of gold comes forth, at the leaping wellspring.*

> **21.** 一、三割高下〔あら〕〔ば〕驚〔か〕ずむかふべし。
>
> 〔天明本〕

*If there is a thirty-percent high-low, do not be startled — face
it.*

*(Annotation: "Tenmei recension.")*

> **22.** 二刻から三刻の高下のある時はいつでも、米にむかふ理としれ.

*Know it as principle: whenever there is a two-step-to-three-step
high-low, face the rice.*

> **23.** 損商内不〔に〕応〔ず〕ならすべし.

*The loss-trade should be accepted without response.*

> **24.** 一、桶商力も、平商〔ひ〕も、みな〔釣〕のゑを喰わせておいて、〔釣〕等ぐるなり.

*The bucket-trading force and the flat-trading alike — all have
first eaten the bait of the hook, and then hung by it.*

> **25.** 三割の高下に徳は転変し、損はならすが秘密なりけり.

*At the thirty-percent high-low, profit reverses; to accept the loss
without resistance — that is the secret.*

> **26.** 万人が万ながら強気なら、たわけになりて米を売べし.
>
> 〔米大崩大変不可驚可買：天明本〕

*When ten-thousand of ten-thousand are strong-spirited, become the
fool and sell the rice.*

*(Annotation in classical Chinese, Tenmei recension: "Great rice-
collapse, great change — do not be startled; buy.")*

> **27.** 野も山も皆〔厭〕めんに弱気なら、あほうになりて米を買べし.

*When field and mountain alike are all turned out and weak-spirited,
become the dolt and buy the rice.*

> **28.** 分別にも思案もいらぬ子〔ひ〕有は、人捨ころ、米くづれなり.

*The child-case in which neither discernment nor deliberation is
needed: the rice-collapse of the human-castaway.*

### ◯ Five-day through Half — *A bridging annotation* (五日通半ヨメス)

*[A short heading in red circle that the OCR renders indeterminately
as `五日通半ヨメス`; the sense is that the following pair of verses
read across a five-day pivot.]*

> いつとても、買落城の弱〔気〕〔か〕とらげ〔か〕、〔こ〕は〔或〕いは〔こ〕うを買〔ひ〕みよ、〔ら〕いこゝ.

*At any time whatever, when the bought side is in collapse with the
weak force in full play — either sell out or try a buy at the
top-price; either will do.*

> いつとても、売落城の〔高気〕とうげ〔か〕、〔こ〕はいこ〔ら〕を売〔る〕がごくいて.

*At any time, when the sell side is in collapse with the high force
at its peak — this is the fullness of selling. *

> 豊卑のくずるゝ米は買みよし。尚〔の〕〔高〕きをま〔ち〕て立〔つ〕耳は〔あ〕られ〔ば〕、ど〔ん〕ごろ〔あ〕〔らけ〕〔ば〕、うるべし.

*Rice that collapses out of the abundance-year is good to try
buying. If a further high still stands as an ear, then at the
burst-open-ness, sell.*

> 米やすく、人気の弱き日は買て、人気の強き〔所〕〔の〕書き日そころ.

*In the days of cheap rice and weak sentiment, buy; the high-day of
strong sentiment is the turning-ridge.*

> 一、只、こめは人気の弱〔気〕日は買て、人気の強〔気〕るにとうるべし.

*Simply: on days of weak sentiment in rice, buy; in times of strong
sentiment, sell.*

> 〔底〕は春〔の〕二刻高下に向ふべし。夏は三刻に向ひ、〔秋〕に向ひに〔向〕〔ふ〕.

*The bottom is to be met at the spring two-step high-low. In summer
meet the three-step; in autumn, meet again.*

> 千人が千人ながら弱けるは〔買〕へ。万人強き日にそうるべし.

*Where a thousand of a thousand are weak-spirited, buy. Sell on the
day that all ten-thousand are strong.*

### 三俵鞘直照可〈レ〉逆 — *Three-tawara spread-price, the reversal rule*

*(Annotation: "Tenmei recension.")*

A brief boxed rule with a *kundoku* return-mark (〈レ〉, **re**)
directing the reading to invert: 照〈レ〉逆 → *reverse and reflect*.

> 二十一日夜十〔々〕者曰く、夜〔生〕者曰く、此〔の〕事 ——

> 一、世後を法とする故、三倍は一刻半〔なり〕、上〔流〕理と、な〔れ〕人ごとに随て〔医〕士さやにもの〔は〕はこの種まひ申.

*The commentator of the twenty-first night says — the night-life
commentator says — of this matter:*

*Because we take the next world as our law, three times is one
step and a half. In the principle of the upper current, following
each person, the physician in his spread is of this very seed.*
