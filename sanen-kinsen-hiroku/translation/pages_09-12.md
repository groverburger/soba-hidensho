# Pages 9–12: The Fifteen Prohibitions and the Position Diagrams

These four pages close the enumerated-maxim section of the book and
open its diagrammatic core. Page 9 closes out the Thirty-Eight
Articles and enumerates the **Fifteen Prohibitions** (十五ヶ条禁制
/ 十五首). Pages 10–11 present the book's first major position-
building diagrams, keyed to the late-fifth-month and sixth-month
pivots. Page 12 presents the two principal method-figures —
**half-plank** (半板) and **at-ease** (安楽) — in tree form.

---

## Page 9 — Remaining maxims; the Fifteen Prohibitions

The page opens with the final four maxims of the preceding Thirty-
Eight-Article sequence — on capital management and the discipline of
the half-plank — and then breaks into the **Fifteen Prohibitions**
formal section.

> 公にても三割さげは米くづれ、万天元のかひ旬を知れ。
>
> 一　中の飲米は、日々に二万俵、一箇月には六十万俵なり。
>
> 京大阪場ふし見の飲米は、二箇月には十五万余。
>
> ふところに金をたやさぬ覚悟せよ。金は米釣しほと知るべし。

*Even at the public level, a 30% decline is a rice-collapse — know
the buying season at the myriad-origin.*

*The middle-range drinking-rice* (飲米, *nomi-gome*, i.e. consumption
rice) *is twenty-thousand* tawara *per day, sixty-myriad per month.*

*The drinking-rice of the Kyoto–Osaka markets and of Fushimi* (場ふし見
→ 大阪場・伏見) *runs over fifteen myriad in two months.*

*Make it your resolve never to let money run dry in your breast.
Know that money is the ebb-and-flood of rice.*

*[These lines give the book's only explicit daily-and-monthly
volume figures for the three principal markets. They also set up
the capital-management argument that follows.]*

> 文珠でも備へのこゝらぬ商内は、宣下の変別あれどやぶは。
>
> 一　売買はいはさの備へも同じこと。米等きなひの軍兵はかね。

*Even a Manjushri, if he trades without backing in reserve, will
find no reprieve in any imperial edict.*

*Buying and selling are the same as provisioning for the shrine of
Iwasa: the soldiery of rice-trading is gold.*

### ◯ Fifteen Prohibitions (十五ヶ条禁制 / 十五首)

*[OCR: the section header is transcribed in the manuscript as both
`○十五禁言ナ` and `十五首`. The received scholarly reading is
十五ヶ条禁制 — the *Fifteen Articles of Prohibition* — with the
variant heading `十五首` (*Fifteen Verses*) immediately beneath.]*

A sub-header `十四` — **Fourteenth** — appears in red ink above the
first prohibition, continuing the enumeration from the previous
section.

The fifteen entries are short negative injunctions, each beginning
from a market condition and ending in a "trading is forbidden"
(禁制, *kinsei*) clause.

> 〔壱〕　侍へなき商内ならば、いつれにても損徳なしに商内禁制。

*When a trade is without its proper attendant, then whether or not
any gain or loss may be, trading is* forbidden.

> 〔弐〕　大法が、秋冬五月はた禁制、春六七月はかいはきむぜい。

*The great rule: in autumn, winter, and the fifth month, [selling
without the pattern] is forbidden; in spring, the sixth and seventh
months, buying is forbidden.*

*[The OCR here reads `かいはきむぜい` — kana transcription for
「買ひは禁制」 ("buying is forbidden") — with a syllable-boundary
split error. The sense is plain.]*

> 〔参〕　豊作は万人気弱く、我もよわし。秋に至りて売は禁制。
>
> 〔四〕　凶作は子気強く、亦強し。多きに〔あり〕て買は禁制。
>
> 〔五〕　弱気理世に現れおれば、皆弱気、安きによつて場は禁制。
>
> 〔六〕　強気が現れ出れば、皆強気、高きにまへて買は禁制。

*An abundant harvest: the multitude grows weak-spirited; I too grow
weak. Coming into autumn, selling is forbidden.*

*A lean harvest: the minor-spirit is strong, and stronger again;
when rice is plentiful, buying is forbidden.*

*If weak-spiritedness has appeared in the world's reasoning, all are
weak — trading by way of cheapness is forbidden.*

*If strong-spiritedness has emerged, all are strong — buying in
advance of a high is forbidden.*

> 〔七〕　上がり足、見ばかく見ゆる米ならば、〔年〕は強みも四は禁制。
>
> 〔八〕　下がる理と、手にとるやうに思ふるも、秋植のうへの湊して禁制。
>
> 〔九〕　五月米、人気弱くて直は上る。早々強けまで売は禁制。
>
> 〔十〕　下が理とたりしく思うに、米にても四五月頃の〔は〕こる〔わ〕刻。

*When the rising step is so visible that even the rice itself seems
to say so, though the year is strong, [selling] in the fourth
month is forbidden.*

*When the reasoning of a decline seems to fall into your very hand,
the autumn planting's confluence is forbidden.*

*Fifth-month rice — sentiment weak, yet the price rises. Until the
strength is clearly in, selling is forbidden.*

*When the reasoning of a decline seems perfectly clear, in rice it
is in the fourth or fifth month that the crash-step comes.*

> 一　天性の理は水無月に出るとしれ。米に随ひ〔平来〕禁制。
>
> 洪水や大風吹きの飛び等なりは、いつのとしでも買は禁制。
>
> 米はづれに買落〔状〕の〔土〕み〔せ〕ぎは、気そまつくとも〔端〕は禁制。

*Know that the natural principle emerges in Minazuki (the sixth
month). Follow the rice; what runs counter to it is forbidden.*

*On great floods or great winds sending rice spiking upward — in
any year, buying is forbidden.*

*When the market breaks and the bought side collapses downward, the
scraping-away that results — even if the spirit settles — [is
forbidden].*

*[OCR for the fifteenth article is fragmentary; the reading is a
best-fit to the visible characters.]*

## Page 10 — The Autumn-Moon Diagram: an 800-*ryō* buying ladder

From here forward the manuscript shifts from verse and maxim to
diagram. Page 10 presents the first of the book's principal figures:
a position-building ladder for the **autumn harvest-moon trade**
(秋名月商内) combined with the **two-hundred-tenth-day trade**
(二百十日商内).

### ◯ The Favorable / Adverse Flat-Trade Methods (順逆平乗商内用)

> **大黒天神秋名月商内并二百十日商〔内〕**
>
> **買桶三平二来半板安楽商内**

*The Daikoku-deity's autumn-harvest-moon trade, together with the
two-hundred-tenth-day trade.*

*Using the buy-bucket, three-flats, two-comings, half-plank, at-ease
method* — (*kai-oke sanpei nirai hanban anraku shōnai*).

> **口伝に曰く。**
>
> *An oral tradition* (口伝, *kuden*) *states:*
>
> **秋名月の商内には、此の図を定木として売買すべし。**
>
> *In the harvest-moon trade, one ought to use this diagram as the
> square-rule and trade by it.*

## Page 12 — The Half-Plank and At-Ease Method Diagrams

Page 12 presents the two principal method-diagrams of the book's
first half. Both are laid out as **four-stage sequential cycles**,
each stage marked in the manuscript by a **red-circled numeral**
① ② ③ ④ placed next to the action at that stage. These are not
branching trees — they are the fixed sequence of buys and sells
that the trader executes as the market moves up and down through
a single cycle.

### ◯ Half-Plank Diagram (半板商内之図)

Four stages, alternating 1000-*ryō* buy and 500-*ryō* sell:

```
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │    ①  千両買   (1000-ryō BUY)            │  initial position
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │    ②  五百両売 (500-ryō SELL)            │  partial unwind on a rise
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │    ③  千両買   (1000-ryō BUY)            │  re-enter on pull-back
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │    ④  五百両売 (500-ryō SELL)            │  partial unwind on a rise
   └─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Each cycle is a pair of scaled entries and partial unwinds. Net
across the full four stages: 2000 bought, 1000 sold → 1000 held
long at reduced average cost. The name **half-plank** (半板,
*hanban*) refers to the half-unwind at each up-move — the trader
is always running with half his plank still underfoot.

*[Verified against manuscript image: the red-circled ① ② ③ ④ are
visible at the four stage positions; the underlying labels read
一・千両買 / 二・五百両売 / 三・千両買 / 四・五百両売 respectively.
The line-stream OCR (`五百両売 / 五百両売 / 四 / 千両買 / 千両買 /
一`) captured the labels but not the circle markers or their
geometric placement.]*

The explanatory prose reads:

> あき日、子雨買、五分〔に〕一割飛び等ぶり、こるはんぶん仕舞。
> 是れ半板なり。
>
> 半々五分〔が〕一刻さがるとき、五百両買〔に〕々〔転〕灰。
>
> 此の商力の仕方を、買桶半板商ゆといふなり。

*On an autumn day, in a child-rain, buy — and as the spike runs
five-bu-to-one-koku, settle out the half. This is the* half-plank.

*When the half-of-a-half (五分 = 5%) declines by one step, reverse
into a 500-*ryō* buy.*

*This trading method is called the* buy-bucket half-plank trade
(買桶半板商ゆ).

> 半板商力も右同意なり。高下に利徳あり、米の本体をうしなはず
> して、天下一のめづらし、其の徳等きにこと、しるべきなり。
>
> 亥更に金のやすらかる半板商拝ても起ても徳これるなり。

*The half-plank method is of the same sense. There is gain in both
rise and fall, without losing the essence of rice — it is the
rarest method under Heaven, and its profit is far beyond the
ordinary. Know this.*

*When one's gold is at ease at the pig-hour's change, by the
half-plank trade — whether one rests or rises — profit is gained.*

### ◯ At-Ease Diagram (安楽商内之図)

The at-ease figure is the half-plank's scaled-down, net-flat analogue.
Same four-stage sequential cycle, but all legs at the 100-*ryō* scale
and alternating buy/sell at the *same* size — so the net position is
flat at the end of each full cycle:

```
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │    ①  百両買   (100-ryō BUY)             │  entry
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │    ②  百両売   (100-ryō SELL)            │  exit on up-move
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │    ③  百両買   (100-ryō BUY)             │  re-enter on pull-back
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │    ④  百両売   (100-ryō SELL)            │  exit on up-move
   └─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Net across the cycle: 200 bought, 200 sold → flat. The method
captures the oscillation between the 5%-to-one-step bands on
either side of a reference price without carrying any overnight
directional exposure — hence **at ease** (安楽, *anraku*).

*[Verified against manuscript image: the four red-circled numerals
① ② ③ ④ are visible at the four stage positions of the figure,

captured the labels without their placement; the sequential cycle

The explanatory prose reads:

> 取図の如く、米等がり足なれば、買桶の出業とはだめ。
>
> 一あきる〔り〕ぶ五分〔に〕一刻とかき日、商内のこらず売りぱらひ、
> 徳をとりまた五分〔が〕一刻さがるとき、もとの如く買取、早た
> 高きる売払ふ。
>
> 此の商内の仕方を、買桶の……

*According to the diagram: when the rice's run-off foot is steady,
the buy-bucket's outgoing move is the final answer.*

*On a fall-off day of 5-bu-to-one-koku, sell out without remainder,
take the profit, and when it declines by another 5-bu-to-one-koku,
buy again as at first; when it rises swiftly, sell out.*

*This method of trading is called the buy-bucket's …*
