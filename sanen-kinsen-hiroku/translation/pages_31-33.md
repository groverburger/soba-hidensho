# Pages 31–33: Afterword and Back Cover

These three pages close the book. Page 31 is the final page of the
main text — a brief restatement of the three first-tier family
secrets and the closing **金泉秘録** marker. Page 32 is the
afterword, in dense flowing cursive, dated *Kaei 4, fifth month,
third day* (the third day of the fifth month of 1851) and signed by
**Narukawa Shigeraku** (鳴川繁楽). Page 33 is the back cover — worn,
woodblock-printed with faint floral decoration, bearing no text.

---

## Page 31 — Closing of the main text

The page concludes the body of the manuscript with the three
distilled family-secret rules of the method, then the closing
title marker.

### The three first-tier family secrets restated

> 毎年、初通段〔を〕中墨と見る — 是、第一の秘伝なり。

*Every year, read the opening trading-day level as the middle-ink
— this is the first secret.*

> 早々、中墨を買申〔さ〕け — 第二なり。

*Buy the middle-ink at once — this is the second.*

> 正金、百両にて百両の商〔印〕を、初発に〔打〕つる — 第三秘伝なり。

*At the outset, stake the one-hundred-ryō trade-seal in true gold of
one hundred *ryō* — this is the third secret.*

*[These three principles are presented on page 30 as the closing of
the weak-qi section; on page 31 they recur as a distilled summary,
forming the book's effective final teaching before the closing
title marker.]*

### Further closing observations

> 一、陰米・陽米ということあり。あがる米を陽、くだる米を陰という。
> 其の理は、陰は陽へくごる、陽はすみ〔直〕のぼるなり.

*There are such things as yin-rice and yang-rice. Rising rice is
yang; falling rice is yin. Yin rolls into yang; yang rises straight
upward.*

> 大阪、長休日の後、相立〔ち〕候ふ松場〔の〕荘のとまり・松場
> 〔の〕あり。

*After Osaka's long holiday, the pine-market residence and pine-
market itself both recur.*

> 多き時は、景気、根強き米と知るべし。〔空〕き時は、不景気と知る
> べし.

*When volume is heavy, know the rice as solid-ranged. When it is
thin, know the conditions as poor.*

> 一、男仕る之内、人々工夫のこへ、しかゝる商内なりは、兎角、切場
> にしこ〔と〕が小べし.

*In the midst of one's manly duties, among many people's devising
of voice, the trade that comes to hand — all considered — one
should take the cut-point as a small thing.*

> 一、東国・西国、おほいに高く、当地も景気〔ある〕時、木〔か〕け
> ておほあげいたし、けへば、極めて売申すべし。安き方も同断.

*When the east and west countries are greatly high and the local
place also has strength, riding up on the climbing-tree — sell out
decisively. The cheap side is handled likewise.*

> 新米相場は、他処の詮〔議〕、〔文〕おほま高について、よし.

*The new-rice market: proceed by reference to the consultations of
other places and, in broad terms, their high.*

### Closing title marker

The page closes with a framed title-marker — a typographic "endmark"
of the same kind that opens each named section of the book:

> **金〔泉〕秘録**

*The* Gold-Spring Secret Record.

## Page 32 — Afterword by Narukawa Shigeraku

The afterword is set apart from the body of the text — on its own
leaf, in dense flowing cursive, on a separate paper that has taken
heavy water- and insect-damage. Only a portion can be read with
confidence from the OCR; much of what follows is a **reconstruction
of apparent sense** rather than a character-by-character translation.

*[Interpretive. OCR for much of page 32 is fragmentary; the cursive
here is the densest in the book, and the paper is visibly worm-
eaten along the outer margin. The rendering below captures what is
legibly recoverable — the opening invocation, the self-critical
rationale for publishing, the dedication and date, and the signature
— without filling in the unrecoverable intermediate passages.]*

### The opening

> 今の世〔に〕〔於〕て〔…〕〔対〕して〔…〕遅れけれ・けれを見ずに
> 歎〔きし〕の前は〔…〕こまに葉文、〔…〕

*In the present age, faced with [the needs before me], the lateness
of the work — [I have seen much that] is lamented before it has
even been seen — letters on fine papers, [matters now reduced to
…].*

### The central concern: fear of error in passing the method down

> 索の坂〔を〕〔超〕えるも見えて、まつときはなしに、事あやまり
> おほきを法に、左てと春買にかくる時は、左とへにいふ一歩千里の
> たがひとなり、たすけ〔を〕と是ならずして、かへりておほきなる
> 木やまち〔い〕となるべし.

*Even as I see the climbing-slope of the search, without a moment's
rest, I have taken as my standard the many errors of matters; when
the early-spring buying is applied in that way, it becomes, as the
saying has it, a "one-step error, thousand-*ri* deviation" — and,
without helping, reversed, it turns instead into a great tree-land
mistake.*

*[The classical idiom **一歩千里のちがい** — "a single misplaced
step, a thousand-*ri* deviation" — is used here as a governing
proverb for the whole editorial concern: the book is a secret-
transmission text in which small errors propagate catastrophically.]*

### Why the publication now

> 玉づれ四五箇年〔の〕娘〔な〕こまり、人々の写してもた〔さ〕るゝ
> 本ども、あま〔り〕老〔の〕かりみけ、〔一〕ひきかへ差〔ら〕へ
> く〔ら〕へみよしと、おもはなく方〔へ〕をあげて、出生〔の〕と
> はならぬ.

*Over these four or five years [of study], with the limited daughter-
of-time, the [corrected] manuscripts that people have been copying
and passing on — many have grown too old and would be the opposite
of right; wishing to pull one out, compare, and set things right, I
have put my hand aside, but the present-life-edition cannot yet be
called settled.*

> されとかく申す、〔我〕 — 此の道にいりそめてより、木〔ま〕このとし
> をつまさ〔れ〕ば、猶、牛田大介もとえ〔ら〕まれ、こ〔の〕事にたが
> ひたるもあらむか、とおそるゝになむ.

*And yet, as I say — ever since I entered upon this path, the
wood-bark years of my own life have gathered, and I still fear
whether I may have, in selecting Ushida Daisuke as the groundwork,
introduced some deviation from his intent.*

*[The name **牛田大介** (Ushida Daisuke) appears here; whether this
is a variant or hon-name of **牛田権三郎** (Gonzaburō) — the named
author of the colophon on page 3 — or a separate figure in the
Ushida transmission line is not made explicit in the surviving text.
The uncertainty is flagged rather than smoothed: if Daisuke and
Gonzaburō are the same man, this is the afterword's clearest
evidence of that fact; if they are different, the editor's worry is
about his choice of source among multiple Ushida transmissions.]*

### The appeal to the discerning reader

> 具眼の人こそ、もし〔かる〕もの〔な〕れ — 〔公〕な〔る〕六もあらば
> た〔か〕しき方に山を通し、あらんめてみかし.

*It is for those with seeing eyes — if any such remain — to take
the matter up: if any public court or six [witnesses] can be found,
run the path to the heights and put it to the test.*

### Date and signature

> **嘉永四年五月三日**

*Third day of the fifth month, Kaei 4* — **May 2, 1851** in the
Gregorian calendar.

> **鳴川繁楽**

*Narukawa Shigeraku.*

*[The signature **繁楽** (*Shigeraku*) differs from the editor-name
**猛之助** (*Takenosuke*) given on the printed colophon of page 3.
These are likely pen-names of the same editor: *Shigeraku* the
literary-personal style, *Takenosuke* the formal editorial name
used in the printed front matter. The afterword is the editor's
private voice; the colophon is his public one.]*
