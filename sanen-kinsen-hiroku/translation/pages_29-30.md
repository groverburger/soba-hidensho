# Pages 29–30: The Effect of Strong and Weak Qi, and the Weather Almanac

These two pages present the interpretation of the **Upper-Three /
Lower-Three Diagram** from page 28, organized as a pair of mirror
sections: **強気の功** — *the effect of strong qi* — where adverse
weather drives prices up, and **弱気の功** — *the effect of weak qi*
— where political and social shocks pull them down. Between the two
sits a compact monthly weather almanac keyed to the lunar calendar.

---

## Page 29 — Reading the Diagram; the Effect of Strong Qi; weather by month

### Interpretation of the Third Diagram

> 此の図を、度端の極意として、中央、方円を形象、天地〔の〕めで
> 〔あり〕。
>
> 東西・南北には四方に、乾坤〔の〕四つの隅を合せて、〔表〕次十分
> 発見。

*This diagram — take it as the secret of the random-edge. The
center, in square-and-circle, figures heaven and earth.*

*East-west and north-south are the four directions; adding the
four corners of ken and kon brings the total to ten points of
discovery.*

*[The figure's reading-scheme: four cardinal + four diagonal = eight
points, plus the center "heaven-earth" box and its ambient field,
giving the **十分** (ten-tenths) discovery-scheme referenced
throughout the book's range calibrations.]*

### Buying side: natural disasters raise prices

> 買方には、旱魃・風雨・天災・買しめの理、春夏秋冬に土きて強気
> ある人の理おほし。

*On the buying side: the principles of drought, wind-and-rain,
heavenly disaster, and buying-enclosure — across spring, summer,
autumn, and winter. Many are the reasons for strong spirit among
those who take these as their ground.*

### Selling side: waiting for the enclosure to break

> 売方には、買しめの崩をまつより外に、一理もなし。故に、此の図を
> あらはすに、一割の強みある理なり。

*On the selling side: there is not one principle other than waiting
for the buying-enclosure to collapse. It is for this reason that,
when the diagram is laid out, there is a one-tenth strength bias on
the buying side.*

*[This is the book's empirical claim: the range-diagram is not
symmetric. Natural disasters (drought, flood, wind, storm) give the
bullish case many independent triggers; the bearish case has only
one — the break of a prior bullish squeeze. The market's structural
drift is therefore weakly upward.]*

### ◯ The Effect of Strong Qi (強気の功)

A monthly weather almanac keyed to the expected agricultural
damage:

> 風雨はげしき時は、四季ともに船の破損、荏土・大阪ともに船間ある
> べし。人〔々〕こゝろえ、第一なり。

*When wind and rain are violent, there are ship-wrecks in every
season; Edo and Osaka alike have gaps in shipping. The first
principle is that each person takes heed.*

> 風烈の時は、四季ともに大事と心得べし。

*At times of violent wind, know it as a critical matter across all
four seasons.*

**By month:**

> 一、三・四月より八・九月までの内、風雨はげしき時は、淡水・田畑
> 〔被〕害〔の〕ことなり。

*From the third or fourth month through the eighth or ninth month:
when wind-and-rain are violent, the effect is flood damage to fields
and paddies.*

> 一、四・五月の旱魃、うゑつけなし。

*Fourth or fifth month drought: no planting.*

> 一、四・五月頃の長雨、麦に〔腐〕〔り〕入る、塩きり、いつば青木し。

*Long rains around the fourth or fifth month: rot enters the barley;
the salt-cut comes; the ground-wood stays green.*

> 四・五月の大風は、麦にあたるべし。

*A great wind of the fourth or fifth month strikes the barley.*

> 六・七月の大風は、惣じて雑穀にあたるなり。

*A great wind of the sixth or seventh month strikes the miscellaneous
grains generally.*

> 六・七月は、旱魃は惣じて畑物にあたる。

*In the sixth or seventh month, drought strikes field crops
generally.*

> 九月の風は、稲にあたる。秋の季にいりて、きす事も、油断ならず
> — 五ヶ秋なり。

*A ninth-month wind strikes the rice. Upon entering the autumnal
season, do not let your guard down at the ripening — this is the
five-element autumn.*

> 八・九月の夏雨、糯くさり。五・六月の長雨ふり、冷気半年々秋〔ま
> で〕の害合ありくるべし。

*Summer rain in the eighth or ninth month rots the glutinous rice.
Long rains in the fifth or sixth month bring cold that can extend
half the year into a damaging autumn.*

## Page 30 — The Effect of Weak Qi

The page opens with one final line on the strong-qi side, then
presents the **weak-qi** mirror.

> 世の中積のこと、弱気に八分に十八里にては、強気には〔六〕分〔に
> 〕誰とつもる。是こがひに勝負なしの中黒り〔続く〕なるべし。

*As for the reckoning of the world: eight-tenths at eighteen *ri*
into weak qi, then strong qi is reckoned six-tenths-to-whom. This
is the continuing middle-black with no victor between the two.*

> 右、強気の功、かくの如し.

*The effect of strong qi, as above.*

### The ideal "full ten-tenths" year is rare

> さて、植はけより、早魃・風雨の天災もなく、十分の世の中といふは、
> 百年に三箇年ならではなるべからず。

*Now — without damage at the planting, without the heavenly disaster
of drought or wind-and-rain — what one may call a world at the full
ten-tenths cannot come to be more than three years in a hundred.*

> 我が国、豊卑なりとても、東西南北の国は吹こたる国もあるべし。
> 前一箇目の豊平を見て、日本国中よかるべしとかならず〳〵思ふへ
> からず。

*Even if our own country is abundant, there may be countries to
east, west, south, or north that have been blown over. Do not — do
not — assume, from one view of a single abundant-peace-year, that
the whole of Japan will be likewise.*

> 惣じて、下直なる国よりさきへあがる物なり。考ふべし。

*In general, it is from the cheapest country first that prices rise.
Consider this.*

> 買方には、すきならし物などを買あげて、米をあくる理もあたが、
> うりには、天性の理をいた〔だ〕広〔く〕あり、外に一理もなし.

*On the buying side, there are reasons even for buying in the plows
and the mundane goods and sending rice higher. On the selling side,
one has only the natural principle, with nothing else to rest on.*

> 買方に、一刻の強みあること。能々差ふべし.

*The buying side has its one-step strength. Consider this closely.*

### ◯ The Effect of Weak Qi (弱気の功)

> 大高直段の耳々〔に〕、□酒など御目〔お〕あまい〔が〕あるはー、
> 〔御〕仰出シ〔に〕あるべし.

*At the very ear of a great-high price, there will be proclamations
on sake and the like — imperial notices.*

> 其外、□また町中御勘門など、仰出〔しの〕あるべし。是また、考ふ
> べし.

*Besides these, there will also be proclamations of town-inspections
and the like. Consider this too.*

### Rumor and counter-rumor

> 或は、吹もせぬ風を吹きたりといひ、〔少〕しの破損も〔十〕分のや
> 〔う〕に、いつはり買〆などいたす年も〔あり〕.

*Or — a wind that did not blow is said to have blown; a minor
damage is spoken of as though it were the full ten-tenths; and
there are years when feigned buy-ins are performed.*

> 日本は神国にて、〔正〕〔直〕をもとする国故、天性の天よりも
> あるべし.

*Japan is the country of the gods; it is a country that takes
uprightness as its root, and so the natural heaven is present
above it.*

> 強気の風雨のいひたり、〔尤〕なれば、船に〔乗る〕気を見て、ほし
> 候〔様〕へは、破損もなく、難なれば入船なり.

*When a "wind-and-rain of strong-qi" is talked up as reasonable, take
the boat-mood into account, and — when the conditions dry out — there
is no damage, and in difficulty the ship makes port.*

> 風はげしき時は、火災をおそれて、蔵結〔ぶ〕にす.

*When wind is violent, fearing fire, one knots the storehouse shut.*

### The three first-tier secrets of the method

The page closes with three numbered principles presented as the
**first-tier family secrets** — the method's distilled opening rules:

> 毎年、初通段〔を〕中墨と見る — 是、第一の秘伝なり。

*Every year, read the opening trading-day level as the middle-ink
— this is the first secret.*

> 早々、中墨を買申け — 第二なり。

*Buy the middle-ink at once — this is the second.*

> 正金、百両にて百両の商〔印〕を、初発に〔打〕つる — 第三秘伝なり。

*At the outset, stake the hundred-ryō trade-seal in true gold of one
hundred *ryō* — this is the third secret.*

### ◯ Yin-Rice and Yang-Rice (陰米陽米)

A short closing annotation:

> 一、陰米・陽米ということあり。あがる米を陽、くだる米を陰と〔い
> ふ〕。其の理は、陰は陽へくごる、陽はすみ〔直〕のぼるなり。

*There are such things as yin-rice and yang-rice. The rice that
rises is yang; the rice that falls is yin. The reason: yin rolls
into yang; yang climbs directly upward.*

### Osaka closing-rules

> 大阪、長休日の後、相立〔ち〕候ふ松場〔の〕荘のとまり・松場〔の〕
> あり。

*In Osaka: after the long holiday, the standing of the pine-market
and the holding of its pine-market-residence both recur.*

> 多き時は、景気、根強き米と知るべし。〔空〕き時は、不景気と知る
> べし.

*When volume is heavy, know the rice as solid-ranged. When it is
thin, know the conditions as poor.*

> 一、男仕る之内、人々工夫のこへ、しかゝる商内なりは、兎角、切場
> にしこ〔と〕が小べし.

*In the midst of one's manly duties, among many people's devising
of voice, the trade that comes to hand — all considered — one should
take the cut-point as a small thing.*

### Closing dynamics to watch

> 一、東国・西国、おほいに高く、当地も景気〔ある〕時、木〔か〕けて
> おほあげいたし、けへば、極めて売申すべし。安き方も同断.

*When the eastern and western countries are greatly high and the
local place also has strength, riding up on that climbing-tree —
sell out decisively. The cheap-side matter is handled likewise.*

> 新米相場は、他処の詮〔議〕、〔文〕おほま高について、よし.

*The new-rice market: proceed by reference to the consultations of
other places and, in broad terms, their high.*
