# Pages 5–8: Favorable and Adverse Years; the Verses of the Four Seasons; the Thirty-Eight Articles

These four pages move from the closing lines of the preface into the
formal opening of the main text. They establish the book's central
distinction — between **favorable years** (*junrai*, 順来) and
**adverse years** (*airai*, 逢来) — and present the manuscript's two
principal mnemonic structures: the **Verses of the Four Seasons**
(*Shiki kōan no uta*) and the **Thirty-Eight Articles of Trading**
(*Sanjūhachi-kajō shōnai*).

---

## Page 5 — Closing of the preface, attribution, and main-text opening

The preface concludes with a brief reciprocal description of adverse
years, mirroring the *junrai* definition that closed page 4.

> 米すくなくして、高き直段を新米へうつしたるの歳は、空腹下のある
> 年なり。六七八月に強気いつれども、まさしく空腹下の歳なり。是を
> **逢来**の歳といふ。

*In years when rice is scarce and the high price transfers to the
new harvest, there is a downward force of markets. Even if a strong
rally appears in the sixth, seventh or eighth month, the year is a
year of downward force nonetheless. This is called a year of*
**adverse conditions** (*airai*, 逢来).

*[OCR throughout this section reads 変来 for what context demands as
逢来 — the same 変/気 confusion noted on page 4 carries through, and
逢/変 also confuse in cursive. In each instance below where the OCR
shows 変来, the reading is 逢来.]*

The author then enumerates the body of his method by name — a
table of contents in miniature:

> 売買の定式は、〔逢平・順来〕の二つなり。〔…〕順来逢来十二平
> 商内、家伝高安鑑、三十八ヶ条商内、十五ヶ条禁制、万歳運気豊凶
> 録、等。

*The settled form of trading divides into two: the favorable and the
adverse. … Together they comprise: the* Twelve-Cycle Trading of the
Favorable and Adverse Years; *the* Family-Tradition Mirror of Highs
and Lows; *the* Thirty-Eight Articles of Trading; *the* Fifteen
Prohibitions; *the* Annual Record of Fortune-Qi and Plenty-and-Want
— *and the rest.*

> 是れを考ふる時は、千度に千度負けざる妙の術なり。誠に家伝秘蔵
> の宝なり。秘すべし秘すべし。

*Studied through, this is the wondrous art that does not lose once
in a thousand attempts. Truly, the secret treasure of the family.
Keep it secret, keep it secret.*

### Attribution

> **宝暦五年秋九月下旬**
>
> **慈雲斎　牛田権三郎**

*Late ninth month, autumn of* **Hōreki 5** *(1755).*

*Jiunsai (慈雲斎) — Ushida Gonzaburō.*

The pen-name **慈雲斎** (*Jiunsai*) appears here, immediately above
Ushida's given name. This is the only place in the book where the
pen-name is recorded. The date — *Hōreki 5*, **1755** — places the
text's original composition nearly a century before the 1851
revised printing recorded in the colophon on page 3, indicating that
Narukawa's edition is the print recension of a much older manuscript
tradition.

### ◯ Main-text opening — *Twelve-Cycle Trading of the Favorable and Adverse Years*

> ○順来逢来十二平商内　〔天明本〕

*The Twelve-Cycle Trading of Favorable and Adverse Years.* *Tenmei
recension* (i.e., this section descends from the **Tenmei-era version
of the text**, 1781–1789).

The first sub-section is set in verse, six lines per verse, alternating
between favorable and adverse years across the three principal seasons
(autumn-into-winter; spring; the early-summer fifth month).

#### **Favorable year — six verses on autumn-and-winter highs and lows**

*Verses describing the seasonal price pattern when old rice is
plentiful and harvests are abundant:*

> 一　秋やすく、空腹上り、一刻半、霜月さがり、暮上るなり。
>
> 一　春わりて、四五月下り、水無月と文月の米下るものなり。

*Autumn cheap; an upward force; one and a half steps; the eleventh
month falls; the year-end rises.*

*Spring breaks; the fourth and fifth months go down; the rice of the
sixth (Minazuki) and seventh (Fumizuki) months goes down.*

#### **Favorable year — six verses on the trading method**

> 秋は買ふべし、空腹上りに売り転変、吉月転変、暮は安楽。
>
> 春転変、四五月は買ひ、五月末はこの種、土け、六七八の暮。

*In autumn, buy; on the upward thrust, sell out and reverse; turn
again on the auspicious month; the year-end is at ease.*

*In spring, reverse; in the fourth and fifth months, buy; at the end
of the fifth month, sow this seed; settle by the end of the sixth,
seventh, and eighth months.*

*[The middle column 「五月寸ゑはこの種土け」 is partly OCR-corrupted;
read as 「五月末はこの種、（？）」 — the verb is unrecoverable from
the OCR alone.]*

#### **Adverse year — six verses on autumn-and-winter highs and lows** (古米すくなき)

> 秋たかく、空腹下り、一刻半、〔霜〕月やすく、暮にもあがらず。

*Autumn high; downward force; one and a half steps; the eleventh
month is cheap; even at year-end it does not rise.*

## Page 6 — Continuation of the seasonal verses; the Mirror of Four-Season Highs and Lows

> 春にわき、順気の歳は、五月さげ、水無月文月上るものなり。

*A favorable-spirit year breaks in the spring: the fifth month
declines, then Minazuki and Fumizuki rise.*

#### **Adverse year — six verses on the trading method**

> 一　秋ははたがら、空腹下しに買ひ半板、霜月折祭、暮休むべし。
>
> ◯　春に〔け〕た四月にのせて、五月すゑ買の種まけ、六七八の暮。

*In autumn, the standing crop falls; on the downward push, buy a
half-plank position; on the eleventh month's mid-festival, rest
through year-end.*

*Spring gives way at the fourth month; at the end of the fifth, sow
the buying seed; settle by the end of the sixth, seventh, and eighth
months.*

### ◯ Family Tradition: Mirror of the Four-Seasons' Highs and Lows — *Tenmei Recension, Volume Two* (家伝四季高安鑑　第二天明本)

This second mnemonic frame restates the same matter as song-verses
(歌, *uta*), grouped not by year-type alone but by season.

> **順来秋冬の高下を考へ歌**
>
> 古米おほく豊年と見る安米は、空腹の上り年と知るべし。

*Verse for considering the autumn-and-winter highs and lows in a
favorable year:*

*When old rice is plentiful and you read the year as abundant, know
the cheap rice as a year of upward force.*

> **逢来秋冬の高下を考ふる歌**
>
> 古米すくなく、強気〔分〕出て、高米は、空腹下りの耳と知るべし。

*Verse for considering the autumn-and-winter highs and lows in an
adverse year:*

*When old rice is scarce and strong-spiritedness pushes out, know the
high rice as the harbinger* (耳, lit. "ear") *of a downward year.*

> **順来逢来春の高下の考へ歌**
>
> 青あげと見て買ふ、〔上屋〕の米はなかなか安くなるまじ。
>
> 春さげと見て、直の安き冬の米、春はなかなか安くなり留まじ.

*Verse for spring's highs and lows in favorable and adverse years
alike:*

*When you read it as a green rise and buy, the rice from the upper
storehouse will not easily turn cheap. When you read it as a
spring decline, the cheap winter rice will not easily settle low
in spring either.*

The page concludes with three further verses on March stability,
on the principle of half-plank (半板) positions, and on the
trader's discipline of patience in the fifth month — including the
maxim that gives the chapter its name:

> 五月米、人気弱くて直は上る。子々孫々まで売は禁制。

*Fifth-month rice — sentiment is weak, yet the price rises. To the
children and the children's children: selling is forbidden.*

## Page 7 — The Thirty-Eight Articles of Trading

This page opens the formal enumerated treatise that is the structural
backbone of the book's first half.

> 一　平あげて翌から、五月高米は、水無月文月さがる年なり。
>
> 逢来夏の高下を考ふる歌
>
> 一　平あけて春から、五月出来は、水無月文月あがる年なり。

*[Final verse-pair on summer pricing, closing the four-seasons verse
sequence: a favorable year that opens flat from spring will see fifth-
month rice fall through Minazuki and Fumizuki; an adverse year on the
same opening will see those same months rise.]*

### ◯ Thirty-Eight Articles of Trading (三十八ヶ条商内)

The OCR for this header reads `三十八首` and then `三十八来商内`
together; the section is plainly the **Thirty-Eight Articles**, the
canonical enumerated treatise referenced both in the preface and in
the table-of-contents above. The articles are individual short
maxims, each opened by 一 (*one*).

#### Articles 1–4 — direction-of-the-year reads, and the spread (鞘) signals

> 一　順来の歳は、空腹後上りなり。秋名月に買ひの種をまけ。
>
> 一　逢来の年は、空腹後さがりなり。秋名月に売りの種をまけ。
>
> 下鞘が上鞘になる鞘がはり、空腹後上り、徳のりにかへ。
>
> 上ざやが下ざやになる鞘がはり、空腹後下り、徳のりにくれ。

*A favorable year: a downward force followed by a rise. At the* harvest
moon (秋名月) *of autumn, sow the seed of buying.*

*An adverse year: a downward force followed by a further fall. At the
harvest moon of autumn, sow the seed of selling.*

*When the spread (鞘, sayagahari) flips from below-spread to
above-spread, after the downward thrust comes the rise — close out
your position on the gain.*

*When the spread flips from above to below, after the downward thrust
comes the further decline — close out your position on the gain.*

a section-recension marker (`天明本〔の〕…`).]*

#### Articles continuing — 五月下旬, the late-fifth-month pivot

> 上ざやが下ざやに〔なる〕、五月すゑから売こねをまけ。
>
> 一　天性の利は水無月に出るとしれ。安ともに米にしたがへ。
>
> 下るべき米、こからあがるなら、徳のこるほど徳のりにかへ。
>
> 五月下旬、高くは、はこの種をせけ、六七月は徳のりにうれ。
>
> 一　五月下旬、安くは、早山の種をまけ、六七月は徳のりにかへ。

*When the above-spread flips to below-spread, from the end of the fifth
month sow the seed of selling.*

*Know that the natural profit emerges in Minazuki (the sixth month);
follow the rice's own movement, low or high.*

*If rice that should fall instead rises from a low, close out as much
gain as remains on the gain.*

*At the end of the fifth month, when prices are high, restrain the
seed of accumulation; in the sixth and seventh, sell out on the gain.*

*At the end of the fifth month, when prices are low, sow the early-
mountain seed; in the sixth and seventh, close out on the gain.*

#### Articles on the broken-market and the wind-and-flood spike

> 一　米はづれ、買落〔状〕の飛びさげして、眼を小ざぎ買の種まき。
>
> 米がれに売落〔状〕の飛びさげは、たわけになりて、はこの種まき。
>
> 一　洪水と大風吹きの飛び上りは、あほうになりて、はこの種まき。

*When the market breaks and the bought side collapses into a
plunging fall, narrow the eye, and sow the seed of buying.*

*When in a depleted market the sold side collapses into a plunging
fall, become the fool, and sow the seed of accumulation.*

*When a great flood or a great wind sends the market spiking upward,
become the dolt, and sow the seed of accumulation.*

*[The verb `はこの種まけ` recurring here is read as "sow the seed of
the (basket) accumulation" — i.e. begin to build a contrarian
position. The phrasing is consistent across the three articles.]*

#### Articles on signal-strength, sentiment, and the fifth-month rise

> 一　五月米、人気弱くて直は上る。四月下旬に小山の種ゝ第。
>
> 強気声にあらはるゝ耳は、米下るべき種なり。
>
> 弱気〔いか〕があらはれ出れば、皆強気了簡なしに売の種まけ。
>
> 弱気〔よ〕世にあらはれぬ年は、米上るべき理なり。

*Fifth-month rice — sentiment weak, yet the price rises. In the late
fourth month, [build a small position].*

*The ear catching strong-spiritedness in the public voice is itself
the seed of rice's coming fall.*

*When weak-spiritedness has emerged into the open, everyone turns
strong-spirited without judgment — sow the seed of selling.*

*The year in which weak-spiritedness fails to emerge into the world
is one in which rice ought to rise.*

## Page 8 — Articles continued; sentiment, money, and the half-plank rule

This page completes the Thirty-Eight Articles with a final cluster
of maxims on the public's mood, on capital management, and on
position-sizing.

> 弱気理世にあらはれなれは、皆弱気、何時にても買の種符。

*When weak-spiritedness has appeared in the world's reasoning, all
turn weak-spirited — at any time, the receipt of buying.*

> 萩の泰子郎殿〔上りまはうけて〕、二割あがらば、こりの種まけ。

*[The phrase 「萩の泰子郎殿」 — possibly a proverbial figure or a
nicknamed market personality — is unattested in the standard rice-
trade vocabulary; the OCR confidence is acceptable but the meaning
is opaque. Apparent sense:* *if a rise is taken on, sow the seed of
the basket once a 20% gain has been made.]*

> 秋の米、空腹下り、火ちかけて、一刻半から二割さげかへ。

*Autumn rice, downward force, with fires near at hand: from one and
a half steps to a 20% decline, reverse.*

#### The rhythm of public sentiment

> 秋やすく、人気も弱く、我もまた売る、ときときときときと。
>
> 秋こかく、人気も強く、我もまた買たき時々と、米の売じゆひ。

*Autumn cheap, sentiment weak, I too would sell, the moments come
and come and come.*

*Autumn rough, sentiment strong, I too would gladly buy, the moments
come and come — and the rice's selling-ripeness is at hand.*

*[The repetition `ときときときときと` is an oral-mnemonic device
characteristic of the verse-articles. Read literally as "the time,
the time, the time, the time…"; rendered above to preserve the
cadence.]*

> 強気の出るとしはなり、選び出して、二百十日にかひの種まけ。
>
> □僧曰、万歳運気豊凶録等を考へて、強気〔森と〕して、二百十日に
> 買の種をまけといふ歌の意なり。

*The year when strong-spiritedness emerges is set: select it, and on
the* two-hundred-tenth day **(二百十日, *nihyaku-tōka*)** *sow the
seed of buying.*

*A monk (?) says: studying the* Annual Record of Fortune-Qi and
Plenty-and-Want, *one cultivates the strong-spirited stance and on
the two-hundred-tenth day sows the seed of buying — this is the sense
of the verse.*

*[The two-hundred-tenth day from the start of the lunar year falls
in early September — the seasonal pivot mark used throughout the
book as a buying signal.]*

> 上り足見はかく見ゆる米ならば、とむと、強気を止めて売るべし。
>
> 一　常弱気、損とくしらぬ大こはけ、貧乏神の氏子なるらむ。
>
> 一　桁灯と鐘と見まこりとよひ、背徳おほし、売損おほし。

*If a rice whose climbing pace is plainly visible is in front of you,
halt — stay your strong spirit and sell.*

*The man who is forever weak-spirited, knowing nothing of loss or
gain — call him a great timid one, a parishioner of the poverty god.*

*[The third maxim here is OCR-fragmentary; the apparent sense is a
warning against confusing chime-and-lamp [household] signals with
trading signals — many betray the principle, many lose on selling.]*

> □金づまりは、いつにても米に随ひ売の種まけ。

*When money is tight, at any time, follow the rice and sow the seed
of selling.*

> □□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□□

#### The half-plank, the four-season spread, and the closing maxims

> 一　大法が、五月下旬が売の約み、秋名月が買のしゆむなり。
>
> 万人が心に構ふ来ならとつれなき道へおもむくがまし。
>
> 一　四季ともに、鞘がはりに土気をさむ、立安ともに米に三たがへ。
>
> 買米を一度にかには無分別。二度に買べし、二度に売べし。
>
> 一　売買に徳の桑たる商内は、半数商力のすくい場とみれ。

*The great rule: late in the fifth month is the appointed time of
selling; the harvest moon of autumn is the ripening of buying.*

*If the year accords with what the multitude expects, better to set
out for an unfrequented path.*

*In every season, on the spread-flip, calm the earth-spirit; whether
firm or easy, follow the rice in three turns.*

*To buy your rice in a single stroke is rashness. Buy in two; sell
in two.*

*The trade in which profit hangs from the mulberry-bough is the
relief-stage of the* half-plank position (半板, *hanban*).

> 百坪に九十九年の高安すは、三刻こゑぬ物と思るべし。
>
> い一にても刻あげては九万一分、千天元のうり旬としれ。

*Across a hundred *tsubo* of land, ninety-nine years of highs and
lows do not exceed three steps — take this for fact.*

*Even by a single move, when the step has risen, it is nine-myriad,
one *bun* — know it as the selling season at the celestial origin
of a thousand.*

*[The closing two maxims of page 8 introduce the term* **天元**
(*tengen*, "celestial origin"), *the foundational reference price
that recurs in the position-sizing diagrams of pages 10–12.]*
