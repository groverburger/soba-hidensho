# Pages 13–16: The Reversal Diagram and the Two-Hundred-Year Almanac

These four pages complete the book's diagrammatic core and open its
long empirical almanac. Page 13 presents the **Reversal Diagram**
(転変商内之図) — the third of the four-stage cycle figures — and
its explanatory prose. Pages 14–16 present the **Record of Fortune-
Qi: Plenty and Want** (万歳運気豊凶録) — a year-by-year
categorization of the sexagenary (干支) cycle by expected weather
and harvest conditions, compiled across *213 years* of observations.

---

## Page 13 — The Reversal Diagram and the prose of reversal-trading

### ◯ Reversal Diagram (転変商内之図)

The third of the four-stage cycle figures. Same geometric layout as
the half-plank and at-ease diagrams on page 12 — four red-circled
numerals ① ② ③ ④ marking the order of operations — but with a
different size relationship between the buy and sell legs:

```
   ┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
   │    ①  千両買   (1000-ryō BUY)            │  entry
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │    ②  二千両売 (2000-ryō SELL)           │  double-size sell on rise
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │    ③  千両買   (1000-ryō BUY)            │  re-cover on pull-back
   ├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
   │    ④  二千両売 (2000-ryō SELL)           │  double-size sell on rise
   └─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Net across the cycle: 2000 bought, 4000 sold → **2000 SHORT** at the
end. Where the half-plank keeps the trader long-biased and the
at-ease keeps him flat, the reversal figure flips him from long to
short within a single market cycle. The ② and ④ legs are each
**twice the size** of the buy legs: the first ②-sell unwinds the
①-buy *and* opens a short of equal size; the ④-sell then does the
same with respect to the ③-buy.

① ② ③ ④ are visible, and the OCR confirms the labels
一 千両買 / 二 二千両売 / 三 千両買 / 四 二千両売. The OCR's
tangential phrase "二千両売一" is a line-stream artifact — the "一"
is the stage-marker of the adjacent cell caught mid-line.]*

### Explanatory prose (left page)

The prose on the left page of the spread names the three companion
methods and describes the reversal in terms of the trader's
psychology.

> 転等商内にいふ、売桶案商内に付、同壱匁引事〔を〕扱ふ。商内より
> 徳別おほし。徳別おほく、常に一心やすからな。福徳円万の世、楽商
> とみれ。

*In the reversal trade — which is the sell-bucket's counsel-trade —
the trading is of the same* one-monme *style. It yields much greater
profit than an ordinary trade. When the profit is great, the heart
grows unsettled. See the world as one of perfect fortune, and trade
at ease.*

> 右図の如く売買するを転変といふなり。米の本解にきのゝはらずめに、
> もの強弱に順に、転変商内の秘密なり。

*To buy and sell according to the diagram at right is called* reversal.
*Without straining against the fundamental nature of the rice, following
the strong-and-weak force of the thing in sequence — this is the secret
of reversal-trading.*

> 口時曰く、買桶転変、売桶転変のふく〔二〕つなり。買桶転変の商内
> の仕方は、木ぶり足の米と見〔立〕てたらぞ、買をおほく、売をすく
> なく商内すべし。

*The oral transmission (*kuden*) states: the reversal has two forms —
the* buy-bucket reversal *and the* sell-bucket reversal. *In the
buy-bucket reversal, once you have judged the rice to be a
timber-rising-foot (i.e. a broadly rising market), trade with many
buys and few sells.*

> 先の仕方は、安きる千両買、五分か一刻たかき〔に〕、三百両売て、
> 三百両売になる。また一五分の一刻〔下〕ある、千三百両買、もとの
> 如く千石買持になる。是を買桶転変といふなり。

*In the method: at cheapness, buy 1,000 *ryō*; at a five-*bu* or
one-step rise, sell 300 — becoming 300 short. Again at a five-*bu*
or one-step move, buy 1,300 — returning to the original 1,000-*koku*
long held. This is what is called the* buy-bucket reversal.

*[The OCR's 「千石」 mid-column reads as 千石 (one thousand *koku*)
— a volume unit — where the surrounding context uses 千両 (a currency
unit). The two units are interchangeable within this passage's
scaling logic: the trader's position is expressed equivalently as
cash or as rice.]*

> 売桶転変商も、右同意のこ〔の〕方〔に〕付〔き〕、伝口伝曰く、売方
> 橋商内〔を〕了簡ちがひと思ふとき、早く見切転発して、強気〔に〕
> なり〔て〕買ふべし。

*The sell-bucket reversal is of the same sense. The oral transmission
says: when you judge that the sell-side bridge-trade has lost its
reason, cut out quickly, reverse, turn strong-spirited, and buy.*

> 一心た〔す〕まて此〔の〕案にして、徳商〔と〕なるべし。買方損商内
> も同意。千架。

*If the heart stays settled and runs on this counsel, gain will come.
The loss-trade on the buying side is of the same sense. A thousand
racks.*

## Page 14 — Opening of the almanac: storms, floods, and cold years

The page opens with a brief closing flourish to the diagram section,
then shifts into the book's empirical core: the almanac.

> 鋸りの拍子はづする転等の商内〔を〕、から〔ず〕踏となほと身ひゆ。

*The reversal-trade, timed to the rhythm of the saw's teeth — step
into it and you cool the whole body.*

### ◯ The Ten-Thousand-Year Record of Fortune-Qi: Plenty and Want (万歳運気豊凶録)

> **二百十三年、二来半板出染転変商内**

*Compiled from two hundred thirteen years. The two-stage,
half-plank, carry-over-and-reverse trade.*

*[The figure 213 years is clearly legible; this is the empirical
base of observations on which the book's seasonal calendar rests.
The trade-method label — 二来半板出染転変商内 — names the composite
technique the almanac's categorizations support.]*

### 暴風　十二箇年 — *Violent winds, twelve years*

> しかける年なり。

*These are the years they strike.*

The twelve sexagenary years of violent wind-disaster:

> 甲子 (*Kinoe-Ne*) · 丁卯 (*Hinoto-U*) · 丁丑 (*Hinoto-Ushi*) ·
> 辛巳 (*Kanoto-Mi*) · 甲申 (*Kinoe-Saru*) · 辛卯 (*Kanoto-U*) ·
> 甲午 (*Kinoe-Uma*) · 丁酉 (*Hinoto-Tori*) · 丁未 (*Hinoto-Hitsuji*) ·
> 辛亥 (*Kanoto-I*) · 甲寅 (*Kinoe-Tora*) · 辛酉 (*Kanoto-Tori*)

In the side-margin, in red ink:

> **木気鬱発 — 唐陰飛木**
>
> *Wood-qi in concentrated eruption — Tang-shade, flying wood.*

Prose underneath:

> 右にあたるとしは中分の平なり。然れども木気鬱発の変気ありて、
> 七八月の間に火風ふけは大凶年となる。変市〔の〕手〔も〕なけれ〔ば〕
> 豊卑不楽〔か〕。

*The years listed above are in themselves middling-ordinary years.
However, when wood-qi erupts as an aberrant force and fire-winds blow
through the seventh and eighth months, they become years of great
disaster. If the market-shift does not arrive, abundance gives no
ease.*

### 洪水　五箇年 — *Great floods, five years*

> 大風と同断。

*Handled the same as great-wind years.*

The five sexagenary years of great flood:

> 己巳 (*Tsuchinoto-Mi*) · 己卯 (*Tsuchinoto-U*) · 己亥 (*Tsuchinoto-I*) ·
> 己酉 (*Tsuchinoto-Tori*) · 壬戌 (*Mizunoe-Inu*)

Margin note, red ink:

> **太陰濕土**
>
> *Greater yin, damp earth.*

Prose:

> 右にあたるとしき中分の早〔年〕なり。然れども土気鬱発の変気あり
> て、七八月の間に大洪水、田畑を申ぶり損ずれは大凶年なり。

*The listed years are in themselves middling-early years. But when
earth-qi erupts aberrantly and great floods sweep the fields through
the seventh and eighth months, they become years of great disaster.*

### 寒冷　三箇年 — *Cold and chill, three years*

> 一断、断〔ニテ〕曰く、此の処に商内仕方の許なし。考ふべし。

*A break. The break reads: in this place, there is no settled method
for trading. Reflect and consider.*

The three sexagenary years of extraordinary cold:

> 乙丑 (*Kinoto-Ushi*) · 乙未 (*Kinoto-Hitsuji*) · 乙巳 (*Kinoto-Mi*)

Prose:

> 一土に打さるとしは、寒冷はなはだしと肝なり。然れ〔ども〕 …

*Years when the earth is struck — the cold can be fierce, be sharp.
However …*

## Page 15 — Long rains, extreme misfortune, and the twenty-eight abundant years

The page opens with the continuation of the cold-years paragraph,
then proceeds through the remaining categorization.

> 〔然れども〕金気（陽明輝金）、水（太陽寒水）との往復あれ〔ば〕、
> 豊卑となる。

*However, when the alternation of metal-qi (bright-yang shining
metal) and water (greater-yang cold water) runs its circuit, they
become years of abundance-against-lowness.*

### 霖雨　二箇年 — *Continuous rains, two years*

Red-circle marker ◯ above the header.

> 一　夜正〔音〕曰く、此の外に商内仕方の注〔に〕し、春〔を〕小〔さ〕
> べし。

*A night-correct-sound notes: apart from this, the prescribed method
is to make the spring small [i.e. take only small positions in
spring].*

> 癸未 (*Mizunoto-Hitsuji*) · 癸丑 (*Mizunoto-Ushi*)

A pair of small annotations in finer hand:

> 一　校正者曰く：○西林和名「ナガアメ」（*nagaame* — "long rain").
>
> 一　今按ずるに又、連爾又名「ニクリョウ一同例」云ふ。
>
> 両三日〔ヲ〕是以下、〔雨ノ〕麓。

*The editor notes: the* nagaame *form (long rain) appears in the*
Saibutsu Wamyō. *In present reading, the same term recurs with a
variant. Two or three days of rain are the foot of this passage.*

Prose:

> 右にあたるとしは寒冷つよく、万物さかえかたき大凶年なり。然れ
> ども、六七月の間に火気欝発の変気あれは、豊早楽楽。

*The years listed above are years of strong cold in which the ten
thousand things cannot easily flourish — great disaster years.
However, if the aberrant force of fire-qi erupts in the sixth and
seventh months, they become years of easy abundance.*

## Page 16 — The abundant-harvest list continued; the tide and wind almanacs

The remaining seven years of the twenty-eight abundant-harvest list
open the page:

> 戊申 · 乙卯 · 丙辰 · 丁巳 · 己未 · 庚申 · 癸亥

Prose:

> 一　右にあたるとしは、勝負鬱発の変気もなら〔ん〕は、翌年なり。
> 然れども六七月に風もふかず、火熱はなはごしけ〔れ〕ば、稲くさり、
> うんか〔の〕つくとしなり。〔能く〕考ふべし。

*The years listed above are, even without the aberrant force of
victory-and-defeat pattern, still the following-year type. However,
if no wind blows in the sixth and seventh months and the fire-heat
is excessive, the rice rots and the swarming insect* (unka) *settles
in — such years. Consider well.*

Margin note, red ink: **温化画** (*On'ka-ga*) — "warm-transformation
figure."

### 中豊作　二箇年 — *Middling abundance, two years*

> 豊作と同断。

*Handled the same as the full-abundance years.*

> 戊午 · 壬辰 · ●校正者曰く：「以上、大周六十年なり」。

*A concluding annotation, red-circled:* *The editor states: the above
completes the great cycle of sixty years.*

Prose:

> 右にあたるとしは、豊平といへども、中分の耳なり。

*Although the years listed are abundant-even, they are only the
middling harbinger.*

### ◯ On the Great and Small Tides (大潮小汐の考周)

> 小しほとは、六・七・八と九と十と二十一・二と三・四・五と知れ。

*Know the* small-tide *days to be: the 6th, 7th, 8th; the 9th; the
10th; the 21st, 22nd; and the 3rd, 4th, 5th.*

> 一　右の外、皆大潮なり。小汐の三日間、大谷の日なり。大風ふきい
> だすなり。風の吹出しはさししほに吹出し、満汐にやむものなり。

*All days other than those listed are great-tide. Three days of
small-tide are the "great-valley" days — this is when the great
winds begin to blow. The wind sets off on the flood tide and dies
at the full tide.*

> 十五日・晦日の三ほは、六ツに満、夜ひるともに九ツにひく。

*On the 15th and the last day of the month, the three low-tides:
full at the sixth hour, and at both day and night pulling back at
the ninth hour.*

> 〽　さししほは日々に四分づとおほく、湯いくも四分づとおそくい
> くなり。

*The flood-tide each day runs four* bu *more, and the ebb runs four
bu *later.*

### ◯ On when the wind blows and does not blow (風吹ふかざるを考用丸)

The page closes with a short practical almanac for predicting
wind-free days — critical knowledge for traders who depended on
coastwise shipping.

> 庚申より十一日の庚午より、去間〔に〕獄、丁丑の日間日なり。
> 以上十五日の間は、風吹ぬものなり。

*From* Kanoe-Saru *for eleven days, and from* Kanoe-Uma *onward — in
the intervening range — with* Hinoto-Ushi *as the central day: over
the fifteen days above, the wind does not blow.*

> 戌寅の日より七日の間、猶〔風なし〕なり。以上十五日の間は、風吹
> ぬにものなり。故に推〔して〕、此の間に、海上に船を乗り出し申す
> なり。万一に〔違〕〔あ〕れば、間〔違〕になり、それも百年のうちの
> 一年と考へしるべし。

*From the* Kinoe-Tora *day, for seven days further, still no wind.
Over these fifteen days of no wind, therefore, by deduction, this is
when one puts ships to sea. If by some chance the calm fails, it is
an exception — know that even such an exception is only one year in
a hundred.*
