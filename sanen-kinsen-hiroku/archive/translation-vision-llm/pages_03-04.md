## Page 3 — Left Side / Page 4 Right Side: Preface Text

### 三猿金泉録序 — *Preface to the Three Monkeys Gold Spring Record*

The Supreme Ultimate (太極, *taikyoku*) moves and gives birth to Yang; when still, it gives birth to Yin. The principle of one stillness and one movement—Heaven and Earth rise and fall according to this logic. Its movement reaches the extreme and becomes still; its stillness reaches the extreme and moves again. Movement and stillness are the root of one another. This is the principle by which strong qi and weak qi alternate.

When this principle is applied to all things, there is nothing it does not encompass. When applied to rice [markets], the qi of people—whether strong or weak—is contained within this principle. Those who combine this principle with the understanding of various people will find it reasonable. In years of good harvests and times of fine weather, one should compare and examine the rice markets. This is the principle beyond mere natural disposition.

## Page 4 — Continuation of Preface and Opening of Main Text

In recent years, new rice arriving in the ninth and tenth months has been declining in price. When rice falls, strong buying emerges—this is the buying pattern. Conversely, when rice has been rising for some months, selling pressure appears at the ceiling. The way of following favorable conditions (*junrai*) and adverse conditions (*airai*): within a period of twelve or thirteen years, favorable conditions last roughly ten years, and adverse conditions three years. Within the high-price years, the secret records of the house, the family tradition of profitable trading arts spanning thirty generations—these and other such [teachings], through the miraculous art of accumulating wealth with a capital of fifteen thousand ryō across ten degrees of trading, are secretly stored.

This is the family tradition of Ushida Gonzaburō.

### 校正三猿金泉秘録 — *Revised Three Monkeys Gold Spring Secret Record*

**On Favorable Conditions (*Junrai*):** Favorable conditions persist for roughly twelve to thirteen years, during which rice is generally at high levels. Ancient rice [old stock], when it is a year of abundance, is cheap, so one should buy in the fourth month, around the middle of the sixth month it will be high, and this is where one sells.

In autumn, one should buy again around the time of the seventh month. By the end of the year, the price of old rice will be high, and one should sell again. This is the pattern of favorable years. In an abundant year, the period of cheapness for old rice extends from around the fourth month through the sixth month.
