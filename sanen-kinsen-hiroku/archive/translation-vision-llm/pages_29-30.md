## Page 29 — Reading the Grand Diagram; The Force of Strong Qi

### 此圖珠寶場の… — *On This Diagram, the Jewel of the Trading Floor*

This page explains how to read the grand diagram from page 28:

The diagram represents the **中星圓珠秘象** — *Middle Star Round Jewel Secret Image*. It combines the seasonal cycle with the trading method, showing at each point in the year whether to buy, sell, or hold. The middle star level is indicated at the center, and the surrounding positions represent deviations above and below.

**天地** (*Tenchi*) — Heaven and Earth: The diagram's upper half represents the Yang months (spring and summer—the period of growth and rising), and the lower half represents the Yin months (autumn and winter—the period of harvest and falling).

**Applying the Diagram:**
- In the four directions (north/south/east/west representing the four seasons), combine the seasonal tendency with the harvest forecast
- When buying in spring: confirm the middle star, then enter at the 10% level
- The rationale for buying and selling at each position is embedded in the diagram
- This diagram is the heart of the entire system

### 強氣の初 — *The Beginning of Strong Qi*

When strong qi (*tsuyoki*) first emerges, the signs are:
- A one-part (*ichi-wari*, 10%) rise from the bottom
- People are still bearish; news is still negative
- Yet the price has stopped falling and begins to creep upward
- This is the critical moment to begin buying

**風雨** (*Fūu*) — Wind and Rain:
Wind and rain are both literal and metaphorical. Literal storms affect the harvest and thus the price. Metaphorical storms—market panics, rumors, sudden disruptions—create the same kind of sudden price movement.

When a storm comes and the ship is at sea, one cannot change the wind. But one can adjust the sails. Similarly, when market conditions shift suddenly, one cannot change the conditions, but one can adjust the position. The four seasons each bring their own storms; the trader who anticipates them is rarely caught off guard.

### Seasonal Weather Observations

- **三月** (Third month): When late frost occurs, the seedlings are damaged; watch for this
- **四月** (Fourth month): The beginning of the growing season; observe rainfall patterns
- **五月** (Fifth month): End of plum rains; if rains continue past this point, flooding is likely
- **六月** (Sixth month): The critical growth period; typhoon season begins
- The combination of wind direction at certain points predicts the autumn harvest with reasonable accuracy

Oil and silk prices follow similar patterns, and the observations apply to those markets as well.

## Page 30 — The World's Accumulation; The Quiet Force; Market Geography

### 世の中積り之事 — *On the World's Accumulation of Matters*

The accumulated wisdom of generations of market observation: approximately one hundred years of records have been consulted. From the Tenmei era backward, through the Hōreki and Meiwa periods, the patterns hold.

**丁** — In certain years of the cycle, the rice market in particular months shows consistent behavior. These are not rules to be followed blindly, but patterns to be weighed alongside current conditions.

The relationship between the rice price and the broader economy: when rice is expensive, all things become dear. When rice is cheap, the economy loosens. The lords (*daimyō*) who depend on rice revenues are affected directly, and their spending in turn affects all markets.

### 弱氣の初 — *The Beginning of Weak Qi*

The counterpart to strong qi—when weak qi (*yowaki*) begins:
- A one-part decline from a sustained high
- People are still bullish; good news continues
- Yet the price stops rising and begins to soften
- This is when selling should begin

The symmetry is exact: just as strong qi begins silently amid pessimism, weak qi begins silently amid optimism. The master trader reads both transitions.

### 大壱落修の事 — *On the Great First Decline*

When a major correction begins, its characteristics:
- The first drop is sharp and frightening
- Panic selling follows
- But the first major drop is rarely the final bottom
- After the panic subsides, a temporary recovery occurs
- Then the slower, grinding decline continues to the true bottom

This pattern—sharp drop, bounce, slow grind—repeats across all markets and all eras. Patience is required to wait for the true bottom rather than buying the first panic.
