### The Method of the Inner Market (*Shōnai no Hihō*)

Within the market, when buying at the level of 1,000 ryō, one must have already planned the selling point. The secret of the inner method is this: when buying positions reach their target, one begins to build selling positions simultaneously—this is called the "turnover method" (*tenkan no hō*).

For every 1,000 ryō in buying positions, prepare to sell 2,000 ryō worth. The reasoning: as the market reaches its peak, the momentum of the crowd drives prices beyond fair value. At this point, the disciplined trader sells more than he bought, profiting from the overshoot.

**On the Psychology of Trading:**

When traders buy at low prices and see profits growing, they become greedy and wish to hold longer. When they see losses, they become fearful and wish to sell at once. Both impulses are the enemy of profit. The disciplined trader sells when he wishes to hold, and holds when he wishes to sell—going against his own emotions as well as those of the crowd.

This art is not to be shared lightly. It was passed down within the family and among trusted associates only.

## Page 14 — Sixty-Year Cycle Weather and Harvest Predictions

### 暴風 運気豊凶之録 — *Record of Storms, Fortune-Qi, and Abundance or Famine*

A twelve-year cycle (*jūni-nen*) governs the alternation of fortune and misfortune in harvests. Two hundred and thirty years of records were consulted, tracking the patterns of favorable weather (*seiten*), adverse conditions (*tenpen*), and their correlation with the sexagenary cycle.

**暴 — Violent Storms/Surges:**

Listed years of violent weather and market disruption:
- 甲子 (Kinoe-Ne), 丁卯 (Hinoto-U), 丁未 (Hinoto-Hitsuji)
- 丁来 (variant), 孝久 (Takahisa era)
- 甲戌己, 甲申, 辛卯, 甲午

In these years, rice markets experience extreme volatility; prices may surge two or three times within a single season. Traders must be especially cautious.

**淡水 — Floods:**
- 己巳, 己卯, 己丑, 己亥, 己酉, 己未
- In years containing 己 (Tsuchinoto), water disasters are more frequent
- These bring three-year periods of disruption

### ◯ 零冷 — *Frost and Cold*
- 己巳, 己亥, 己未
- Unusual cold damages crops; three-year adverse periods follow
- Rice prices rise sharply in response

## Page 15 — Harvest Forecasting by the Sexagenary Cycle

### 水勢 — *Water Momentum (Flooding Patterns)*

The cycle of floods and droughts follows certain year-signs:
- 癸未, 癸丑 — years associated with water excess
- The pattern repeats across the full sixty-year cycle

### ◯ 豊雨 — *Abundant Rain*

Years of abundant rainfall (beneficial for crops):
- Two-year periods of good rainfall produce abundant harvests
- All monetary calculations and market positions should account for expected harvests

### 梅立 — *Plum Rains (Rainy Season Onset)*

The onset of the *tsuyu* (plum rain) season, approximately eight years in a cycle:
- 丙寅, 丙子, 庚辰, 庚寅
- 壬午, 壬辰, 丙申, 丙午
- The timing of the rainy season affects summer crop growth
- Early plum rains generally indicate good harvests
- Late plum rains may indicate drought followed by flooding

### 豊作 — *Bountiful Harvest*

Years of expected bountiful harvest, spanning twenty-eight years of records:
- Listed by sexagenary year across the full cycle
- 戊辰, 庚午, 辛未, 壬申, 丁亥
- 戊子, 癸巳, 甲戌, 庚辰, 庚寅
- 戊辰, 庚午, 丙酉, 辛未, 壬申 (etc.)

In autumn months, yields of twenty-eight years' worth of bountiful harvests were tracked and correlated with the cycle years.

## Page 16 — Medium Harvests, Tidal Patterns, and Wind Cycles

### 中豊作旅 — *Medium-Good Harvest Travel/Pattern*

Roughly equivalent to a moderately good year. Correction days (*shōsei-bi*) are noted, spanning three-year periods.

The analysis covers the relationship between the sexagenary cycle and agricultural outcomes, noting that medium harvests of a certain type produce their own characteristic market patterns.

### ◯ 大潮小潮之差 — *Difference Between Spring Tides and Neap Tides*

The tidal patterns of spring tides (*ōshio*) and neap tides (*koshio*) are analyzed in relation to weather prediction. In years of great tidal difference, storms are more likely. The timing of tides in relation to planting and harvesting seasons provides further indicators.

### ◯ 風 — *Wind Patterns*

Wind direction and timing analyzed across the cycle:
- 戊申, 己卯, 丙辰, 丁巳, 庚申 — noted wind-year associations
- In years of strong autumn wind, harvest damage increases
- The period between the seventh and ninth months is critical
- Wind from certain directions at certain times predicts the harvest outcome

A ten-year or fifteen-year span is noted for the wind's major directional shifts. The relationship between wind patterns, tidal patterns, and harvest predictions forms the basis of the *Tenmon* (heavenly inquiry) forecasting system.

The text notes that across one hundred years of observation, these cycles hold with reasonable reliability—roughly one year of error in every ten.
