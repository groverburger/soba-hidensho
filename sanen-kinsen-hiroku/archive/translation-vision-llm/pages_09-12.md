## Page 9 — The Fifteen Articles of Trading; Market Sentiment

### ◯ 十五ヶ条 禁物場 — *Fifteen Articles: Forbidden Things of the Marketplace*

Among the provisions for trading, those who deal in large quantities and those with small capital must both observe the following. Within the market, one must read the sentiment of the crowd—whether people are inclined to buy or sell—and not act rashly.

The large trader (*ōakindo*) may think that because he has capital, he can afford to hold a losing position. This is a grave error. Even with great capital, if one buys recklessly and the market moves against him, the losses compound. Conversely, a trader of modest means who reads the market well and trades with discipline can accumulate wealth steadily.

**On Buying and Selling Judgment (*Baibai no Handan*):**

The decision to buy or sell should not be made lightly. One should observe for several days. When the rice price has been falling and reaches a point where it no longer declines despite bad news—this is the moment to buy. When rice has been rising and ceases to advance despite favorable reports—this is the moment to sell.

Do not follow the crowd blindly. When all are buying, consider selling; when all are selling, consider buying. The profit lies in going against common sentiment, yet one must have sound reason for doing so.

## Page 10 — Trading with 100 Ryō: Favorable-Year Calculations

### ◯ 順道 — *The Way of Favorable Conditions*

**Example: Trading in a Favorable Year**

Starting capital: 100 ryō. Trading old rice (*komai*) from the spring/summer buying season through year-end.

Buy in the third or fourth month at the low level; positions built at intervals:
- 20 tawara (bales) purchased across multiple stages
- By the fifth month, the price has risen; partial sales lock in profits
- Buying again at 7–9 tawara increments at each dip

**百両売 — Selling at 100 Ryō:**
In the Tenmei era, within the market:
- 20 positions held (*nijū-tawara*)
- Selling at 18 stages, then 17 stages
- Adjustments at levels of 500, 800 bales
- Running total through compounding: from 100 ryō initial to 200 ryō, then building further

**Family tradition, twelfth month, 100 ryō within the market.**

### Diagram: 百両売 — *100 Ryō Sell (right side)*

```
        100 Ryō Sell
        /          \
      ②            ①
     /    \
   ④      ③    (stages)
```

The text explains: When the market has risen to a level where selling is indicated, one does not sell the entire position at once. Instead, one sells in stages—first a quarter, then another quarter, observing the market's reaction. If the market continues to rise after the first sale, one waits; if it begins to fall, one accelerates the selling.

This is the secret method (*hihō*) of the house: never commit fully at once, but scale in and scale out, reading momentum at each step. The buying price and selling price must be considered together—what matters is not any single trade but the cumulative result.

When one has accumulated 500 ryō in buying positions and the market turns, begin selling from the top. The relationship between the five-part division and the profit at each level determines the final gain.
