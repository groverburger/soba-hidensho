## Page 31 — Regional Market Intelligence: Osaka, Edo, and the Provinces

### On Regional Price Differences and Information Flow

Each year, the initial rice trading (*hatsu-akinai*) establishes a base level. From this level, observations are made month by month. The cycle of buying at one hundred ryō and selling at one hundred ryō—the method of disciplined proportional trading—applies regardless of the regional market.

The relationship between the great markets:

**大坂長持** (*Ōsaka Nagamochi*) — Osaka's sustained positions:
- Osaka (Dōjima) is the central rice exchange; all other regional prices reference it
- The information from Osaka flows outward to the provinces
- Osaka prices reflect the *accumulated* wisdom of all traders; provincial prices reflect *local* conditions
- The gap between Osaka and provincial prices creates trading opportunities

**東国西国** (*Tōgoku Saigoku*) — Eastern and Western Provinces:
- Eastern provinces (Kantō region) and western provinces (Kansai, Chūgoku) have different harvest cycles
- When eastern harvests are poor but western harvests are good, the price in Osaka reflects the average
- A trader in the provinces who knows the Osaka price has an advantage over one who knows only local prices
- The southern (*minami*) market's tendencies differ from the northern (*kita*)

**豊作** (*Hōsaku*) — On Abundant Harvests:
- In years of abundance, the key is *when* the abundance becomes known
- Early knowledge of a good harvest allows selling before the price drops
- The flow of information—from the fields to the provincial markets to Osaka—takes time
- The trader who can read the signs in the fields before the market knows has the greatest advantage

The text references the relationship between physical geography, weather patterns, and market prices, noting that the same storm system produces different effects in different regions.

## Page 32 — Afterword: The Essence of the Teaching

### Closing Reflections

In all the world's markets, the rice market is the foundation. What has been written in this record is the accumulated observation of generations—the secret teachings of the house, refined and corrected.

The essence of the teaching is this: **the market is not a place of chance but of pattern.** Those who study the patterns—of seasons, of cycles, of human sentiment—can trade with the odds in their favor. But even the most learned trader will sometimes be wrong, and the method of staged entry and proportional trading exists precisely for this reason: to survive being wrong while maximizing the benefit of being right.

One should approach the market with the same virtues that govern all of life:
- **仁** (*Jin*) — Benevolence: patience and compassion for one's own errors
- **智** (*Chi*) — Wisdom: study and observation before action
- **勇** (*Yū*) — Courage: acting decisively when the signs align
- **節** (*Setsu*) — Discipline: following the method even when emotions urge otherwise

The trader who embodies all four virtues will prosper across generations. The trader who relies on any one alone will eventually fail.

This record should not be shared carelessly. It represents a lifetime—indeed, many lifetimes—of observation, and its value lies not merely in the rules but in the *understanding* behind them. A rule without understanding is dangerous; understanding without discipline is useless.

To the reader who has studied this record thoroughly: may you trade with wisdom and prosper.

**鳴川猛之助** — *Narukawa Takenosuke* (editor/corrector)

**嘉永四年五月廿日** — *Twentieth day of the fifth month, Kaei 4* (approximately 1851)
