## Page 27 — Trading Psychology: Patience and the Way of Gold

### 商内 — *On Trading*

The central teaching of this section:

When one has entered a position and the market moves sideways for many days, doing nothing, the impulse to act grows unbearable. This is the critical test. The trader who exits from boredom or impatience will miss the eventual move.

When all things in the market seem to be falling—prices falling, sentiment falling, hope falling—yet the fundamental conditions (harvest, seasonal cycle, middle star level) support the position, one must hold. This is the discipline that separates the master from the amateur.

**On the movement of gold and goods:**

Gold (capital) and goods (rice) move in opposite directions. When gold is plentiful and goods scarce, prices rise. When goods are plentiful and gold scarce, prices fall. This is obvious, yet in the heat of trading, men forget this fundamental law.

### 随金 商内 — *Following Gold in Trading*

The concept of "following gold" (*zuikin*): one's trading should follow where capital naturally flows. Do not fight the movement of gold—instead, observe where it concentrates and follow it there. This is different from following the crowd's *opinion*; rather, follow the actual flow of *capital*.

**On patience and money:**

There is a saying: "The money that was thought lost returns after three years." In trading, positions that seem like losses in the short term often prove profitable in the long term—if the fundamental analysis was correct. The error is not in the analysis but in the trader's inability to endure the interim.

The cautious man who holds 陸金 (land gold, i.e., stable reserves) of twenty tawara and trades with five tawara will survive all storms. He who trades with his entire fortune will eventually be ruined.
