## Page 21 — The Three Levels of the Market (*Yo no Naka Sandan no Zu*)

### Preamble

The text discusses the rice market as an arena of rising and falling prices, where the trader must judge whether the current level is at the upper, middle, or lower range. Without this judgment, all other techniques are useless—one must first know *where* in the cycle the price stands.

The number of rice-producing domains and their yields are tallied:

### ◯ 諸高 — *Total Yields*

Rice combined from all regions:
- Total: 28,548 *chō* (districts) producing rice
- Autumn harvest estimate for all domains tallied: 9,484,815 *tawara*

### 世の中 三段之圖 — *Diagram of the Three Levels of the World*

A circular diagram divided into three bands:

```
    ┌─────────────────────┐
    │    上 (Upper)         │
    │  35 tawara level     │
    ├─────────────────────┤
    │    中 (Middle)        │
    │  30 tawara, 1 bu     │
    ├─────────────────────┤
    │    下 (Lower)         │
    │  25 tawara level     │
    └─────────────────────┘
```

- **上 (*Ue/Jō*) — Upper:** 35 tawara — this is the ceiling range
- **中 (*Naka/Chū*) — Middle:** 30 tawara, 1 bu — the equilibrium range
- **下 (*Shita/Ge*) — Lower:** 25 tawara — the floor range

Additional markings:
- **天井** (*Tenjō*) — Ceiling: the highest price point, at the 25 tawara supply level (scarce supply = high price)
- **底直** (*Soko-ne*) — Floor price: at 35 tawara on the low side (i.e., when supply is 35 tawara per unit, price is low)
- **中星** (*Chūboshi*) — Middle Star: the central equilibrium point at approximately 30 tawara

## Page 22 — The Middle Star System and Secret of Bullish Trading

### The Middle Star (*Chūboshi*) as Reference Point

The "middle star" concept: across thirty years of observation, the average rice price establishes a central level—the *chūboshi*. In years of abundance, price falls below this level; in years of scarcity, it rises above. The key teaching is:

- When price is at the *chūboshi* level: observe and wait
- When price falls to 20% below *chūboshi*: begin buying
- When price rises to 20% above *chūboshi*: begin selling

In a year of abundant harvest (*hōsaku*), the direct price (*choku-ne*) falls to the middle star level or below. One should buy at this point and hold, as in subsequent years the price will revert.

The new rice (*shinmai*) price and the middle star are compared: when new rice arrives cheaply but the middle star indicates the underlying trend is upward, the new rice represents a buying opportunity.

### ◯ 強氣立の秘傳 — *Secret Tradition of Bullish Positioning*

The secret method for establishing bullish positions:
- At 400–800 ryō, begin with staged entries
- At 20% below the middle star: first entry
- At 30% below: second entry (larger)
- The total position should never exceed what can be held for one full year

Across the cycle:
- **壱** (*Ichi*) — First position: small, probing
- **弐** (*Ni*) — Second: confirmed direction
- Continue until fully positioned

**◯ 顧氣** — *Reflecting on Momentum*
