〈IMPORTANT NOTE ON THE AFTERWORD:

Looking at the manuscript: the text IS a closing section, and I can
see dense cursive prose that appears to be a summation. However,
the flowing cursive makes it very difficult to confirm that the
specific English phrasing is a DIRECT translation vs. an editorial
distillation.

What I CAN confirm from the manuscript:
- The closing IS a summation (not new content)
- It is attributed to 鳴川猛之助 (visible at end)
- A date appears near the bottom
- The text is of moderate length (roughly 15-20 columns of cursive)

What I CANNOT confirm character-by-character:
- Whether specific memorable phrases ("the market is not a place
  of chance but of pattern") correspond to specific Japanese
  sentences in the manuscript
- Whether the four virtues are explicitly named as a list in this
  closing section, or whether they are being recalled from earlier
  in the text

The SAFEST assessment: the afterword translation captures the
SUBSTANCE of the manuscript's closing but may be more polished
and structured than the original prose warrants. This should be
flagged as "interpretive translation" rather than "direct
translation" until a more confident reading can be made.〉
