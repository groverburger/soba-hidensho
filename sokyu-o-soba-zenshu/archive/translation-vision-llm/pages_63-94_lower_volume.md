## Chapter 1: The Timing of Declines

### 下巻第一章　落節の事


Declines have their own rhythm and timing
(節, *setsu*). Within each month there are one or two
regular points of decline. Know these timing points and use them to
determine when to buy and sell. The 15th and month-end are common
inflection points.


---


## Chapter 2: A Three-Month Consolidation and Rotating Capital

### 下巻第二章　相場三ヶ月持合金繰り回の事


If the market does not move for three months, exit
and rotate your capital elsewhere. Idle capital earns nothing. During
consolidation, deploy funds to other opportunities. When the market
begins to move again, re-enter.


---


## Chapter 3: Trading by Principle, Not by Gambling

### 下巻第三章


Trade by reason (道理 (*d\=ori*)), not by luck.
Trading without reason is mere gambling. He who trades by reason profits
long-term; he who trades without reason profits only temporarily and
ultimately loses.


---


## Chapter 4: Entering Positions at Low Prices

### 下巻第四章　當買を低下に仕掛の事


The entry price matters most. Buy at low
prices---even a small rise yields profit. Buy at high prices and only a
large rise yields anything. Use previous bottom prices as reference
points for low-price entries.


---


## Chapter 5: The Three-Year Harvest Cycle

### 下巻第五章　三年結構の方へ國を米達の事


Three consecutive years of good harvests lead to
an oversupply of rice and falling prices. Three years of poor harvests
lead to scarcity and rising prices. Know this three-year cycle to read
the overall trend. After abundance comes scarcity; after scarcity comes
abundance---a perpetual cycle.


---


## Chapter 6: The Great Rising Day

### 下巻第六章　天一大上日の事


Certain days mark great turning points in the
market. These are known through the calendar (暦 (*koyomi*)). When
the market moves on such a day, pay close attention. These major-move
days come only a few times per year---wait for them and act decisively.


---


## Chapter 7: Not Trading Without Adequate Capital

### 下巻第七章　金に際なくして思ふ時合ぶない事


Do not trade when your capital is insufficient. To
borrow money to trade or to trade under financial stress impairs
judgment. Trade only with surplus capital. Trading without capital is
like going to battle on an empty stomach---you will have no strength and
will lose.


---


## Chapter 8: The Cyclical Nature of Market Rises and Falls

### 下巻第八章　上下の波の如き事


``Rises and falls recurring'' means that a market which has risen will fall again, and a market which has fallen will rise again. The market perpetually repeats its rises and falls.

``Reaching one's aim'' means that a position one has initiated reaches its objective. Within the repeated rises and falls, it is essential to reach the objective of one's trade.

*Seed:* The recurring rises and falls are like waves. Know the movement of the waves and ride them to take profit. He who opposes the waves will suffer losses.


---


## Chapter 9: The Laws Governing Major Market Moves

### 下巻第九章　大相場の法の事


A ``major market move'' refers to a market of great fluctuation. The movement of major markets has its own laws of momentum. A market that has risen greatly will fall greatly. A market that has fallen greatly will rise greatly. Knowing this law is essential.

*Seed:* The momentum of a major market can extend over several months. Determine the direction of the overall trend and ride its momentum. He who opposes the overall trend will inevitably suffer losses.


---


## Chapter 10: Not Judging the Trend from a Single Day

### 下巻第十章　一日の動きにて大勢判ずべからずの事


A single day's price represents one day's market value. One must not judge the overall trend from a single day's movement. A single day's movement is but a small wave and does not indicate the overall trend.

Do not let your heart be swayed by a single day's price movement. Observe the movement over several days and several months, and determine the overall trend from that. Rejoicing and despairing over each day's rise and fall is not the way of the market.

*Seed:* To trade based on a single day's movement is no different from gambling. Judge the overall trend by reason, and only then act.

## Chapter 11: Trading Twenty-Six Bales and Proper Position Sizing

### 下巻第十一章　二十六俵相場張の事


*Seed:* Twenty-six bales (俵) refers to a quantity of rice, denoting a trade of twenty-six bales. This chapter describes the principles one should know when trading in such quantities.

The principles of trading differ according to whether the quantity is large or small. For large-quantity trades, one should act with a broad mind. For small-quantity trades, one should act with caution.

## Chapter 12: Smaller Quantities and Careful Management

### 下巻第十二章　十七俵少量の事


Seventeen bales is a small-lot trade. Even in small-lot trades, the principles are the same as for large lots. Determine the overall trend and trade in accordance with reason.

Do not be careless simply because the quantity is small. Through the accumulation of small lots, large profits are made.

*Seed:* Whether in large lots or small lots, the way of the market is the same. Act in accordance with reason.

After gaining profit from seventeen-bale trades, gradually increase your quantity. However, do not trade in quantities exceeding your own strength. Determine the quantity according to your means.

Furthermore, even in small-lot trades, keep a ledger. Review the ledger to know the results of your own trading. Compare the times of large profit with the times of large loss, and know your own weaknesses.

## Chapter 13: Trading with One Kan of Capital

### 下巻第十三章　一貫金の張方の事


``One *kan* purchase'' means buying with one *kan* (貫目) of gold. This chapter describes how one should trade when using one *kan* of capital.

*Seed:* When trading with one *kan* of capital, first commit one-third of it to initiate the position, and keep the remainder as reserves. Do not invest the full amount at once.

## Chapter 14: The Golden Ratio of Effort and Profit

### 下巻第十四章　八分の力三分の利の事


``Eight parts effort, three parts profit'' means using eight-tenths of your strength to buy and taking three-tenths profit as sufficient. One must not use the full ten-tenths of one's strength. Use eight and keep two in reserve.

Be content with three-tenths profit. If you try to take the full ten-tenths, you may instead lose your gains entirely. This is as stated in Teaching~14 of the upper volume.

*Seed:* Eight-tenths effort for three-tenths profit---this is the golden rule of the market. Keep strength in reserve and restrain your profit-taking. He who observes this principle will sustain himself in the market for a long time.

## Chapter 15: Not Buying in Abundant Years

### 下巻第十五章　豊年に買ふべからずの事


Do not buy in abundant years. In abundant years, the supply of rice is plentiful and prices normally fall. If you accumulate buying positions in an abundant year, it will be difficult to endure the decline in prices.

Therefore, in abundant years, refrain from buying and look for opportunities to sell. Sell at the high prices of an abundant year and wait for lower prices before buying later. This is the way to handle the market in abundant years.

*Seed:* Even in abundant years, prices may rise temporarily. To ride such a temporary rise and buy is dangerous. Since it is only a temporary rise within an overall decline, prices will soon fall again.


---


## Chapter 16: Not Selling Based Solely on Past Patterns

### 下巻第十六章　因果のみにて賣るべからずの事


Do not sell by comparing with past results (因果 (*inga*)). ``Past results'' means judging based on what happened before. To think that because prices fell in the past they must fall again, and to sell on that basis, is not necessarily correct.

The past is past, and the present is the present. Look at today's overall trend and make today's judgment. To be captive to the past and lose sight of the present is an error in the way of the market.

*Seed:* To trade based on past results is like walking a road while looking behind you. One should look ahead and walk the road. That is to say, look at today's overall trend and trade accordingly.

The key insight regarding past results is that one should look at the overall trend and judge the present from it. Do not be captive to the past; follow today's overall trend. When the overall trend is rising, buy without relying on past results. When the overall trend is falling, sell without relying on past results.


---


## Chapter 17: The Halfway Point After a Sharp Decline

### 下巻第十七章　天井より急落半値の事


A market that falls sharply from the ceiling will decline rapidly to the halfway point (半値 (*hanne*)). From the ceiling price to the bottom price, the midpoint---that is, the half-value---is where momentum is strong and the decline is swift.

*Seed:* During a sharp decline, do not buy until the halfway point is reached. After reaching the halfway point, observe whether the bottom is firm, and only then buy. To buy in the middle of a sharp decline is like catching a falling blade with your bare hand.


---


## Chapter 18: Ceilings and Bottoms in Abundant and Famine Years

### 下巻第十八章　豊年凶年の天井底の事


The way of making the market and the momentum of the trend in abundant years differs from that in famine years. In abundant years, the supply of rice is plentiful and prices naturally fall. At such times, one should take the path of selling.

In abundant years, the ceiling is low and the bottom deep. In famine years, the ceiling is high and the bottom shallow. Know this difference and determine your method of trading accordingly.

*Seed:* In abundant years, do not try to profit greatly. It is best to take small gains steadily. Famine years are the opportunity to take large profits.


---


## Chapter 19: Do Not Chase Profits That Have Fled

### 下巻第十九章　逃げ足を追ふべからずの事


Do not chase profits that flee like running feet. Profits depart swiftly, like feet. In chasing profits, you may instead invite losses. Take profits when they come. Do not pursue them after they have gone.


---


## Chapter 20: Not Following the Crowd

### 下巻第二十章　人に附かぬの事


*Seed:* It happens that one follows what others do and does the same. When others buy, you buy too; when others sell, you sell too. This is moving in the wake of others.

However, he who follows others is always late. If you move after others, your profits will be small. Move ahead of others by your own judgment.


---


## Chapter 21: Not Wagering Your Entire Fortune

### 下巻第廿一章　有金殘らず張るべからずの事


Do not stake everything---that is, do not commit your entire resources to trading. He who commits all his resources to the market will be ruined by a single failure. Always keep reserves on hand.


---


## Chapter 22: Three Tests and Seven Days of Confirmation

### 下巻第廿二章　三度の試しと七日の勢ひの事


Ceilings and bottoms are settled after being tested three times. Do not determine a ceiling or bottom from a single test. Test three times and then judge.

When buying, observe the movement over seven days before acting. If prices rise for seven days, the momentum is strong. If prices fall for seven days, the momentum is weak. Use the count of seven days to judge the overall trend.


---


## Chapter 23: The Three Tiers of the Market

### 下巻第廿三章　上中下三段の事


The ``three-tier market'' means viewing the market in three stages. The upper stage is near the ceiling, the middle stage is at the half-value, and the lower stage is near the bottom.

Buy at the lower stage and sell at the upper stage. This is the great road of the market. At the middle stage, since prices may move in either direction, one should refrain from acting.


---


## Chapter 24: July as a Turning-Point Month

### 下巻第廿四章　七月轉換の事


July is a month when the market turns. The summer decline may find its bottom in July, and prices may begin rising toward autumn.

The bottom is reached in July because the outlook for the new rice crop becomes clear. In years of abundant harvest, prices may fall still further from the July bottom. In years of poor harvest, prices may rise greatly from the July bottom.

The movement in July determines the overall trend for the second half of the year. Pay attention to the July market and set your strategy for autumn and beyond accordingly.

Furthermore, July follows the sixth-month settlement period, and the consolidation that lasted through June may break. Observe the direction of this breakout and trade accordingly.


---


## Chapter 25: Monitoring the Osaka Market

### 下巻第廿五章　上方の相場を見るの事


The ``upper market'' (上方 (*kamigata*)) refers to the \=Osaka market. Know where the \=Osaka market's movement reaches, and use it to judge the Sakata market. The \=Osaka market may move first, and the Sakata market may follow afterward.

*Seed:* It is essential to know the price difference between the \=Osaka and Sakata markets. When the difference is large, it is an easy time to take profits.


---


## Chapter 26: Month-End Settlement Days

### 下巻第廿六章　月末の節日の事


The ``month-end settlement market'' refers to a market that is settled at the end of the month. The month-end settlement market tends to move as the end of the month approaches.

*Seed:* Pay attention to settlement days. From three days before month-end, settlement trading increases and prices may move.


---


## Chapter 27: Comparing October and August Prices

### 下巻第廿七章　十月と八月の値比べの事


It is essential to compare the markets in October and August and know the state of markets in all regions. October is the month after the autumn harvest, when the supply of rice is settled. August is before the harvest, when the outlook is still uncertain.

Compare the prices of these two months and judge the year's harvest quality accordingly.

## Chapter 28: Unfavorable Calendar Days for Trading

### 下巻第廿八章　甲乙の不利日の事


Days of the *k\=o-otsu* (甲乙) cycle are days when the market does not move. On *k\=o-otsu* days, do not initiate large positions. If you initiate trades on days when the market does not move, commissions pile up with no profit.

The market has days that move and days that do not move. Choose the days that move and initiate trades on those days.

## Chapter 29: Significant Dates for Rice Delivery

### 下巻第廿九章　三月六月の節日の事


The sixth and third months have many significant days for rice deliveries. ``Delivery days'' are the days when rice shipments arrive.

## Chapter 30: The Fifth and Seventh Day as Inflection Points

### 下巻第三十章　五日目七日目の節の事


The ``gates of five and seven'' mean that inflection points occur at the fifth and seventh day of a market move. If prices rise for five days, they will pull back once; if they rise for seven days, they will pull back again. Knowing these gates of five and seven is essential.

## Chapter 31: Autumn Is the Time to Buy

### 下巻第三十一章　秋は買ひの時の事


Autumn is the season of the rice harvest, and prices are normally low. Accumulate buying positions at this cheap time. Buy at autumn's low prices and sell at winter's high prices. This is the standard way of the market.


---


## Chapter 32: Tracking Daily Momentum

### 下巻第三十二章　日勢の事


Daily momentum (日勢 (*nissei*)) means the market's momentum each day. Observe the daily momentum and use it to judge the overall trend. Just as weapons and tools are useful only when polished every day, so too with the market. Knowing daily momentum is the foundation of trading.


---


## Chapter 33: Unlucky Days When the Market Is Prone to Sharp Moves

### 下巻第三十三章　厄日の事


Unlucky days (厄日 (*yakubi*)) are days when the market is prone to sharp fluctuation. On unlucky days, there can be large moves. Pay attention to these days and act when you see the opportunity. Also, since great losses may occur on unlucky days, exercise caution.


---


## Chapter 34: Quarter-End Settlement Points

### 下巻第三十四章　四半期の節の事


*Seed:* The end of March, the end of June, the end of September, and the end of December are critically important seasonal junctures. At these month-ends, settlement trading is heavy and the market may move sharply.


---


## Chapter 35: Buying in Three Installments Around Settlement Days

### 下巻第三十五章　節日の前中後三度買ひの事


There is a method of buying in three large installments around settlement days. Buy in three parts: before, on, and after the settlement day. The first purchase is before the settlement day, the second on the day itself, and the third after the settlement day. By dividing into three purchases in this manner, one can prepare for the movements around the settlement.


---


## Chapter 36: Identifying the Day the Market's Direction Is Established

### 下巻第三十六章　立て局の日の事


The ``day the direction is established'' is the day when the market's overall trend becomes settled. On this day, there can be a large move. Identify the day the direction is established and act without letting the opportunity escape.


---


## Chapter 37: Reading the Tail of a Move

### 下巻第三十七章　勢ひの尾を讀むの事


The ``tail'' means the tail-end of the market's momentum. The ``two tails'' are the tail of the rise and the tail of the decline. The tail of the rise is near the ceiling, where momentum wanes. The tail of the decline is near the bottom, where momentum is exhausted.

Discerning these two tails is a matter of great importance in the way of the market.


---


## Chapter 38: Major Moves Begin After Settlement Days

### 下巻第三十八章　節後の大動きの事


*Seed:* That ``great moves begin after settlement days'' means that after a settlement day, the market may begin to move greatly. Since settlement days are turning points in the market, pay close attention to the movement after settlement days.


---


## Chapter 39: The Five Rules of the Rice Merchant's Mindset

### 下巻第三十九章　米商の心得五則の事


The rice merchant's mental discipline is the root of the way of the market. The rice merchant should always keep his heart right and act in accordance with reason. He whose heart is right will gain profit; he whose heart is crooked will suffer loss.

Observe the overall trend, know the seasons, perceive market sentiment, and only then trade. When the heart is unsettled, rest. When the heart is composed, act.

*Seed:* The rice merchant's principles are as follows:

- Act in accordance with reason.
- Abandon greed.
- Observe the overall trend before moving.
- Wait with patience.
- Keep the heart right when engaging the market.

He who observes these five rules will sustain himself as a rice merchant.

## Chapter 40: Approaching the Market with a Single Great Resolve

### 下巻第四十章　一大決心の事


``One great mental resolve'' means having a single, large way of holding one's mind. When approaching the market, unify your mind and think broadly. Do not be captive to small matters; see things on a grand scale.

## Chapter 41: Buying in the Month That Heaven Has Ordained

### 下巻第四十一章　天の定めし月に買ふの事


*Seed:* The month to buy is the month when heaven has set the bottom. Buy at the bottom that heaven has ordained. It is not a bottom determined by human judgment, but a bottom determined by heaven.

The ``bottom set by heaven'' means a bottom determined by natural principles. Through changes in harvest abundance and famine, and through seasonal movements, a natural bottom is reached. Discern this natural bottom and buy.

## Chapter 42: Withdrawing and Saving Market Profits

### 下巻第四十二章　利を引きて貯むの事


Profits from winning should be withdrawn and set aside---that is, the money gained from the market should be taken out and saved separately. If money gained from profits is reinvested in the market, the profits will quickly vanish and turn to losses.

*Seed:* When you gain a profit, be sure to save a portion of it separately. Do not reinvest everything in the market. Save profits and hold them as reserves against times of failure. This is the way to continue in the market for a long time.

This concerns those who invest large sums beyond their means, devote themselves entirely, and bring misfortune upon their lives. That is, those who invest large sums in the market and lose them, ruining themselves.

Those who lose large sums in the market are those who engage in contests exceeding their own strength. Trade within your means and engage in contests that are not unreasonable.

## Chapter 46: Accumulating Capital Gradually

### 下巻第四十六章　少しづつの積み上げの事


One should use money like piling up a mountain, reaching one's goal only afterward. Accumulate capital little by little and build it into a large sum. Do not spend large sums all at once.

*Seed:* In using money, one must observe moderation. He who uses money without moderation will ultimately lose it all.

## Chapter 47: Not Blindly Following Famous Traders

### 下巻第四十七章　名人に盲目に附くべからずの事


Learning the momentum of famous buyers but failing to notice when the market peaks and falls---this happens when one follows the buying momentum of well-known traders without one's own judgment.

To watch others buy and then buy, or watch others sell and then sell, is merely following others and is not one's own path. Act on your own judgment.

*Seed:* Following the movements of a famous market trader may appear safe at first glance, but if you do not know the timing of his exit, you may suffer losses.

## Chapter 48: Market Momentum Is Self-Sustaining

### 下巻第四十八章　勢ひは勢ひを呼ぶの事


The market's momentum simply persists. That is, if there is upward momentum, it continues upward as it is; if there is downward momentum, it continues downward as it is. Do not oppose the momentum.

*Seed:* Riding the momentum is a matter of great importance in the way of the market. He who opposes the momentum will suffer losses. He who follows the momentum will gain profit.


---


## Chapter 49: Carrying Positions Through Month-End Settlements

### 下巻第四十九章　月末を越すの事


This is the method of buying and carrying positions over the month-end to reach one's objective. Carrying positions over the month-end requires both surplus capital and patience.

*Seed:* Carrying positions over the month should be employed only in years when the overall trend is upward. If you carry positions over in a year when the trend is unsettled, you will idle your capital with no profit.

## Chapter 50: The Principle of Water

### 下巻第五十章　水の理の事


The ``principle of water'' in buying means that, like water seeking a low point, one should buy at low prices. ``Reaching one's objective and pulling up'' means withdrawing after reaching your target. ``The ship's error of judgment'' means aspiring to move like a great ship and making an error of the heart.

Just as setting out to sea in a small boat is dangerous, so is entering a major market with small capital. Know the size of your own vessel and act accordingly.

*Seed:* The principle of water is the foundation of the market. Water flows from high to low and naturally becomes level. The market is the same. When prices are high, they naturally fall; when prices are low, they naturally rise. Follow this natural principle.

Furthermore, the ship's error of judgment arises from the greed of wanting to take large profits. Abandon greed and act in accordance with reason.


---


## Chapter 51: Combining Monthly Positions into a Large Holding

### 下巻第五十一章　月々の合せ大張りの事


``Combining monthly positions and drawing momentum'' means accumulating purchases over several months, combining their terms, and building them into a large force of momentum.

Even if each month's purchase is small, combining the purchases of several months makes it large. This method is a way of gradually increasing one's position and is akin to the method of averaging.

*Seed:* The method of combining monthly positions and drawing momentum should be employed in years when the overall trend is upward. If this method is used when the trend is unsettled, it will only enlarge one's losses.

Furthermore, when combining monthly positions, one should know the average purchase price for each month. If the market rises above the average price, there is profit; if it falls below, there is loss. It is essential always to know the average price.

The meaning of this chapter is to describe the method of gradually increasing one's purchases to obtain a large profit. It should be employed only after the overall trend has been ascertained.

## Chapter 52: Monitoring Both the Osaka and Sakata Markets

### 下巻第五十二章　上方と酒田の相場見合せの事


*Seed:* This describes combining the momentum of the \=Osaka market with the reaching momentum of the Sakata market, and planning the deployment of capital accordingly. Observe both the \=Osaka and the Sakata market momentum, and determine how to use your capital.

When the \=Osaka momentum is strong and the Sakata momentum is also strong, buy boldly. When \=Osaka is weak and Sakata is strong, buy with restraint.

## Chapter 53: Keeping One's Heart Right and Following Reason

### 下巻第五十三章　心正しく理に從ふの事


The most important thing in the rice market is to keep one's heart right and follow reason. If the heart is crooked, one will not gain profit. If one acts against reason, one will suffer losses.

The way of the market is the way of humanity. He who observes the way of humanity will observe the way of the market as well. He who departs from the way of humanity will depart from the way of the market as well.

*Seed:* Rice is the foundation of the realm. He who trades in rice is involved in the great affairs of the realm. One must not forget this awareness.


---


## Chapter 54: Reading the Extreme Low Point and Buying Momentum

### 下巻第五十四章　極安と買ひの勢ひの事


*Seed:* The extreme of low prices is the point where the bottom has been reached. Only those who know the direction of market momentum can buy at the extreme low.

The extreme bottom is reached when prices test the bottom three times and fail to decline further. This is the time to buy.

## Chapter 55: Confirming the Bottom's Firmness Before Buying

### 下巻第五十五章　底堅めを見て買ふの事


The ``footing'' of a bottom market is the firm ground of the bottom. When the footing is firm, buy with confidence. When the footing is not yet settled, continue to wait.

*Seed:* Determining the footing is settled when the bottom has been tested three times without breaking. This method is as stated in the upper volume.

## Chapter 56: Accumulating Many Small Wins

### 下巻第五十六章　小勝をかさぬの事


Count and divide your wins, and establish victories to accumulate numbers. That is, pile up winning trades and accumulate profit. Rather than one large win, it is better to accumulate many small wins.

### 下巻第五十七章　月々の天井下がりの事


A market that makes a ceiling every month may be turning to a decline. When each month's ceiling becomes progressively lower, it is a sign that the overall trend is turning downward. Observe this sign and determine the right moment to sell.

Days with the number eight---the 8th, 18th, and 28th---are unfavorable for buying. These are inauspicious days for the market, and it is not advisable to initiate positions on them.

### 下巻第五十九章　三段の構えの事


The ``threefold plan'' means positioning in three layers. Extended over three rounds of trading, one aims to achieve a great profit. That is, by initiating positions across three rounds, one gains a large profit.


---


## Chapter 60: Comparing Mid-Month and Month-End Prices in May

### 下巻第六十章　五月半ばと月末の値比べの事


Compare mid-May prices with month-end prices. When prices at month-end are lower than at mid-month, they will continue falling in June. When prices at month-end are higher than at mid-month, they may continue rising in June.

## Chapter 61: Even a Half-Profit Is a Success

### 下巻第六十一章　半利も首尾の事


Even winning once and taking only half the possible profit may be sufficient to reach one's objective. Be content with half the profit. Do not lose your gains by attempting to capture the full amount.


---


## Chapter 62: Buying at the Midpoint of a Seasonal Cycle

### 下巻第六十二章　季節の半ばに買ふの事


``Buying with a name at the midpoint of the season'' means buying at the midpoint of a seasonal cycle. There is a method of buying at the midpoint without waiting for the seasonal juncture. This method moves ahead of the seasonal juncture, making it easy to gain profit.

## Chapter 63: Aligning the Right Month with the Right Mental State

### 下巻第六十三章　買月と心と合はすの事


*Seed:* This describes the match between the buying month and one's mental state. He who knows the right month to buy and acts with a correct heart will reach his objective. He who mistakes the month to buy, or whose heart is not right, will fail to reach his objective and suffer losses.

## Chapter 64: Year-Long Trading and the Way of the Four Seasons

### 下巻第六十四章　通年と四時の道の事


To trade ``through the year'' means to conduct market operations throughout the entire year. Within a year, there are times of rise, times of decline, and times of consolidation. Act according to each of these periods.

*Seed:* Year-long trading requires both body and mind to be sound. To keep one's heart right and not err in judgment over the long span of a year is an exceedingly difficult undertaking. Yet he who masters this path will gain great profit.

*Seed:* The principles of year-long trading are as follows:

(1) Spring is the time for buying.

(2) Summer is the time for resting.

(3) Autumn is the time for building positions.

(4) Winter is the time for taking profit.

Follow these seasonal principles and trade through the year. Do not oppose the movement of the four seasons. Act in accordance with nature.

With this chapter, the principal teachings of the lower volume are substantially concluded.

## Chapter 65: Reading Agricultural Reports and Weather

### 下巻第六十五章　農報と天氣を讀むの事


There are times when one judges the market based on agricultural reports. The success or failure of the harvest is a matter of great importance in determining the overall market trend. Know the agricultural reports and use them to judge whether the harvest is abundant or poor.

*Seed:* Whether the harvest is abundant or poor depends greatly on the weather in June and July. Observe the weather during this period carefully and use it to forecast the autumn harvest.

## Chapter 66: The Ultimate Teaching: A Right Heart Prevails

### 下巻第六十六章　正心にて終に勝つの事


Even when one enters with a resolute heart, there are times when the momentum does not reach its mark and one cannot win. Even entering with a correct heart, depending on the momentum of the times, there are occasions when one cannot prevail. Yet he whose heart is right will, through the accumulation of many trades, ultimately prevail.

*Seed:* He whose heart is right is not broken by a temporary defeat. Through the accumulation of many victories, he gains great profit. This is the ultimate meaning of the way of the market, and the conclusion of this book.
