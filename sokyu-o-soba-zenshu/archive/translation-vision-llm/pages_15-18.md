## Chapter 1: On Grain Being the Great Foundation of the Nation

### 第一章　水穀は國の大本なるの事

Of all the great treasures under heaven, nothing compares to rice and grain. Of all the things that sustain human life, nothing surpasses it. Therefore, anyone who wishes to master the way of the market must first understand the fundamental importance of grain.

One must grasp(?) this principle, understand the essential purpose of the market, and only then study the methods of trading. Rice is the great foundation of the nation; the people of the realm depend upon it for their livelihood. The price of one *koku* (石) of rice rises and falls according to the quality of the harvest (*hōkyō*, 豊凶). In years of abundance, prices are low; in years of famine, prices are high. This is the natural order of things. Yet the market, beyond this natural order, rises and falls greatly according to the inclination of popular sentiment (*ninki*, 人氣). Through people's speculation, prices rise still higher when already high, and fall still lower when already low. This is what makes a market a market.

---

**(1)** Since the price of rice is thus determined by a combination(?) of the natural principles of heaven and earth and the inclinations of popular sentiment, anyone who intends to trade must first understand these two principles.

In undertaking any trading, one must first assess the overall trend (*taisei*, 大勢) of the realm. Is it a good(?) harvest, or a poor(?) one? What is the condition(?) of the economy? What are the circumstances(?) in the various provinces(?)? One must observe(?) all(?) of these carefully(?) and only then(?) buy(?) or sell(?). To trade(?) without(?) knowing(?) the overall trend(?), relying only on the prices(?) before one's eyes(?), is like a blind(?) man(?) walking(?) a road(?) in the dark(?) of night(?).

**(2)** The market is the human heart. To see into people's hearts is the essential point of the way of the market. When everyone thinks of buying, the price of rice will certainly rise. When everyone thinks of selling, the price of rice will certainly fall. And yet, when everyone is buying, the top is near; when everyone is selling, the bottom is near. Therefore, to go against(?) the crowd's heart is the secret(?) of the market.

**[Editor's note]** This(?) chapter(?) sets forth the fundamental(?) principles(?) of the market. The reader(?) should(?) savor(?) its meaning(?) well and apply(?) it(?) to the chapters(?) that follow(?).

The way of the market is like water flowing to the lowest point. Water flows from high to low and naturally levels itself. The market is the same. When prices are high, they naturally fall; when prices are low, they naturally rise. He who understands this principle may be said to have grasped the way of the market. Yet people of the world do not understand this principle — they go on buying when prices are high and selling when prices are low. This is a great error.

In the market, there is a great upheaval within every three-year period. Taking three years as one cycle(?), there will be changes(?) of harvest quality within that span. Know(?) these changes, buy in abundant years(?) when prices are low, and sell in famine years(?) when prices are high. This(?) is the great way (*taidō*, 大道) of the market(?). Do not(?) forfeit(?) great(?) profits(?) by grasping(?) at(?) small(?) ones.

Furthermore(?), in market(?) trading, one must not listen to what others(?) say(?). Think for yourself(?), judge for yourself(?), and decide for yourself(?). To trade(?) by following(?) what others(?) say(?) will certainly(?) lead to failure(?).

The way(?) of the market is deep(?) and far-reaching(?). It cannot(?) be mastered(?) in a single morning or evening(?). One must cultivate(?) it over(?) years(?) and months(?), studying(?) day(?) and night(?), before(?) one can attain(?) its subtlety(?).

## Chapter 2: On Not Buying Declining Grain at High Prices

### 第二章　下穀は高値にて買ふべからずの事

In trading, one must not buy declining grain (*shitakoku*, 下穀) at high prices. "Declining grain" refers to rice whose price is falling. ## Chapter 3: On the Spirit of Plunging Headlong into Greatly Risen Grain

### 第三章　引は大穀の説大中へ飛込む心の事
