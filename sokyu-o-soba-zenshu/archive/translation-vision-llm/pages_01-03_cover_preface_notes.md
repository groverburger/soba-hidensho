## Page 2 — Preface

### Preface to the Complete Works of Old Man Sōkyū on Markets
### 宗久翁相場全集緒言 (*Sōkyū-ō Sōba Zenshū Shogen*)

Books treating the subject of markets are exceedingly numerous, yet most of them run to empty theorizing, and those which can actually be put to practical use are very few. Old Man Sōkyū was truly a man who may be called a god(?) of the market; the depths of meaning contained in his posthumous manuscripts are remarkably profound, and this is a book that anyone engaged in market trading must keep at hand. Yet until now there has been no complete printed edition of this work — it has survived only through hand-copied manuscripts. Some time ago I collected these, added scholarly annotations, assembled them into a single work, and named it the *Complete Works of Old Man Sōkyū on Markets*. Sōkyū's writings on markets had been scattered and lost, and there was no way to know their full scope; but through many years of collecting(?), I believe I have at last succeeded in gathering the greater part. I have now arranged these into two volumes, upper and lower(?), and commit them to print so as to serve the needs of those in the world engaged in market trading.

A certain month of Meiji 42 [1909]

Recorded by Nikiku Hayasaka

*[Note: The preface is dated 1909, one year before the publication date of 1910 given in the colophon on page 94.]*

## Page 3 — Editorial Notes

### Editorial Notes
### 凡例 (*Hanrei*)

1. This book consists of the posthumous manuscripts of Old Man Sōkyū relating to markets. It is a collection of texts that had previously been transmitted only in hand-copied form, to which editorial revisions(?) have been added.

2. The format(?) of this book respects the original texts as they are, and every effort has been made to preserve the original wording. However, obvious errors and omissions(?) have been corrected, and for the reader's convenience, punctuation has been added(?).

3. For difficult words and phrases, the annotator has provided explanatory notes.

4. This book is divided into two volumes, upper and lower(?). The upper volume contains writings on the principles of trading (*kokoroe*, 心得) and methods of buying and selling. The lower volume contains writings on chart lines(?) (*keisen*, 罫線) and matters relating to timing and seasonality.

5. Market terms(?) that are no longer in common use today have been given special notes(?) explaining their meaning.

6. Several(?) variant manuscript copies of the original exist. Their differences(?) consist mainly of variations in wording(?), with no differences in overall meaning. This book takes the most complete(?) manuscript as its base text and has collated it against the other manuscripts(?).

Recorded by the Annotator
