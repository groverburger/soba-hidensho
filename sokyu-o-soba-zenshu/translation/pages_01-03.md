## Page 2 — Preface (緒言)

> 宗久翁相場全集緒言

### Preface to the *Sōkyū-ō Soba Zenshū*

> 宗久翁遺稿は[OCR: 士挙及位附 → uncertain; likely 一挙に位附]の書、
> 識見高邁着眼奇警、相塲の機微を洞察するの方法を網羅解説して
> 亦遺憾なし、宜なる哉翁が多大の成功を收めたるや、唯翁が手澤
> の原本は秘庫に襲藏せられて容易く見る可からず、又坊間の傳寫
> 本は魯魚[OCR: 百田す → likely 百出す]を傳へ轉訛す、是啻に翁が
> 遺訓の眞意を害するのみならず恐らくは後進を誤ること多大なら
> んとを、吾人之を憂ひ同家に懇請して翁の手澤本を謄寫し尚坊間
> の諸傳寫本を參校し以て數十條を増補し且書中の商用語及難解の
> 處には一々註解を加へ其意義を闡明して一書を述作し名づけて
> 宗久翁相塲全集と云ふ、今や印刷方に成る、聊か一言を卷首に
> 辯じて其由來を明かにす。
>
> 明治四十三年四月　早阪豊識

The posthumous writings of Master Sōkyū are a book of
[OCR: uncertain] market positioning — lofty in insight, striking in
perception, exhaustively explicating the methods by which one
penetrates the subtleties of the market, leaving nothing to be
desired. How fitting, indeed, that the master achieved such great
success. Yet the original manuscript in the master's own hand has
been kept under lock in the family's treasury, not easily seen by
outsiders. Meanwhile, the hand-copies circulating in the world are
rife with scribal errors — characters mistaken one for another,
corruptions multiplying with each transcription. This not only does
violence to the true meaning of the master's teachings but risks
leading future students astray.

Troubled by this state of affairs, we earnestly petitioned the Honma
family and obtained permission to transcribe the master's autograph
manuscript. We further collated it against the various hand-copies in
circulation, thereby supplementing several dozen additional articles.
Moreover, where the text employs trade jargon or presents difficult
passages, we have appended individual annotations to elucidate the
meaning. The resulting work we have titled *Sōkyū-ō Soba Zenshū*
("Complete Collected Works on Markets by Master Sōkyū"). Now that it
has gone to press, we offer these few words at the head of the volume
to make clear its provenance.

April, Meiji 43 (1910). Written by Hayasaka Yutaka (早阪豊,
*Hayasaka Yutaka*), also known as Nikiku (二菊).

## Page 3 — Annotator's Notes (凡例)

> 凡例

### Notes on This Edition

> 一　本書は翁家の秘襲なる自筆の稿本を原とし坊間の傳寫本を
> 以て校正増補し、且註解を加へて其眞意を發揮せんとを勉めたり。
> 然れ共註者の不敏なる、恐らくは、翁が眞意の十が一を傳ふる能
> はざらむとを。特に駈引の妙用賣買の調子等に至つては、筆舌の
> 能く盡す所にあらず、讀者、宜しく意を言外に求むべし。

**One.** This book takes as its base text the autograph manuscript
held in secret by the master's family, corrected and supplemented
by reference to the hand-copies in circulation, with annotations
added in an effort to bring out the true meaning. Yet given the
annotator's own limitations, we fear we may fail to convey even a
tenth of the master's true intent. Particularly when it comes to the
subtle art of market maneuvering (駈引, *kakehiki*) and the rhythm
of trading — these are matters that no brush or tongue can fully
exhaust. The reader is advised to seek the meaning beyond the words.

> 一　本書記する所の月日は、皆太陰暦なり、又當地とあるは、
> 荘内地方のこと、知るべし。

**Two.** All dates recorded in this book follow the lunar calendar
(太陰暦, *taiin-reki*). Wherever the text says "this region"
(當地, *tōchi*), it refers to the Shōnai district (荘内地方).

> 一　本書の原理は、何れの地に於ても應用するとを得れども、
> 翁は、立論の基礎を莊内地方に置きたるが故に、讀者本書の意味を
> 明瞭に解するには。先づ莊内地方米商の慣習を知了するの必要あり、
> 是特に、卷頭に酒田舊米會所の慣習を掲録せる所以也。

**Three.** The principles in this book may be applied in any region,
but since the master based his arguments on the Shōnai district,
a clear understanding of the text requires the reader first to
comprehend the trading customs of the Shōnai rice merchants. This
is precisely why we have included the customs of the former Sakata
Rice Exchange (酒田米會所, *Sakata Kome Kaisho*) at the head of the
volume.

> 一　期米駈引の妙用を悟らむと欲せば、幾遍となく本書を繰返し
> 精讀するとを要す。然らば、自然に相塲の強弱を判別し、駈引の
> 妙用に通することを得ん。特に賣買を仕掛け居る時に臨みては
> 决して讀みを怠る可からず、是れ相塲に對する注意を忽にせざる
> 爲なり。
>
> 校註者復識

**Four.** Anyone who wishes to grasp the subtle art of futures
maneuvering (期米駈引, *kimai kakehiki*) must read this book
through again and again, many times over. Do so, and in time one
will naturally come to distinguish strength from weakness in the
market and master the art of maneuvering. Above all, while one
holds an open position, one must never neglect this reading — for
to do so is to grow careless in one's attention to the market.

Noted again by the collator-annotator.
