## Page 87 — Lower Volume, Chapters 65–66

### Chapter 65: 商急ぐ可からざる事 — *One Must Not Rush a Trade*

> 一商進み急ぐ可からず、賣買共に思入進み候時は、今日より外、商塲なき樣に思ふ者なれども、是は功者なき故也。幾月も見合せ、通を考へ、慥なる處にて可仕掛也。無理に、天井直段底直段の考へなく仕掛る故、手違になる也、是れ急ぐ故也、

One must not rush headlong into a trade. When one has formed a
conviction and presses forward, whether buying or selling, it feels
as though no opportunity will ever come again — but this is the mark
of inexperience. Wait for months, consider the range-bound
oscillation (通, *kayoi*), and initiate the position only at a
reliable juncture. Positions opened recklessly, with no assessment
of the ceiling price (天井直段, *tenjō nedan*) or floor price
(底直段, *soko nedan*), end in failure. This is the consequence
of haste.

**Commentary** (*Shaku*):

> (釋)本章は、上卷第一章の異文である。意味は、已に説てあるから、茲に略する。

This chapter is a variant text of Upper Volume, Chapter 1. Its
meaning has already been explained there, and is therefore omitted
here.

### Chapter 66: 心易き人にも賣買進め間敷事 — *Do Not Recommend Trades Even to Close Friends*

> 一何程心易き人にも、賣買進め申間敷也。若し了簡違ふ時は、恨みを得る也。總て、相塲高下の論致まじき也、此道を心得る人は、我了簡を立てず、人の了簡にて商する人はなき筈也。少々にても圖に当る時は、募り、了簡を立たがる者也。是第一可愼處也。勿論、慥なる高下を見定め打明け人に語り進むる時は、人も其気になる故、二三俵方も利を得る人は、一俵方ならで利運ならざる者也、慥なる處を見据へ候はゞ、人々に拘はらずして、賣買可致候。尤も世の中に隨ひ、諸國作合、豐作凶作、上方相場、九州様子、聞合せ随分宜しき也。存念は、決して人に語る可からず、是大極意也。常々専ら慎むべし。

No matter how close the friendship, one must never recommend trades
to another person. Should the assessment prove wrong, one earns
nothing but resentment. As a general rule, one ought never to debate
whether the market will rise or fall. A person who truly understands
this way (此道, *kono michi*) does not abandon his own judgment to
trade on another's opinion — that is simply not done. Yet even a
small stroke of luck emboldens people, and they grow eager to form
opinions. This is the very first thing one must guard against.

Of course, when one has identified a reliable move in the market and
speaks openly of it, urging others to act, they too become caught up
in the excitement. The result is that a person who might have
profited by two or three tawara ends up unable to profit by even
one. If one has fixed upon a reliable position, one should trade
without regard for others' opinions. Naturally, one should keep
abreast of the times — gathering intelligence on crop conditions
across the provinces (諸國作合, *shokoku sakuai*), good harvests
and bad, the Kamigata (上方, *Kamigata*) market, and conditions in
Kyushu. But one's own views must never, under any circumstances, be
disclosed to others. This is the supreme secret (大極意,
*daiokui*). One must be vigilant about this at all times.

- **12th month, 2nd day**: 24 tawara [OCR: 丑日辛天井百四十俵上げ → likely 五日辛天井; a *kanoto*-stem ceiling, 140-tawara rise]
